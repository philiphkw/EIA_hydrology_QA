SECTION 10: STABILITY AND REACTIVITY
Chemical Stability Chemically stable under normal conditions (room temperature)
Conditions to avoid High temperatures (decomposes above 1500°C).
Exothermic reaction with: Amines, Potassium Permanganate, Halogenates, Semimetal Oxides, Hydrides
Semimetals, Aldehydes, Vinylmethyl ether. Danger of ignition or formation of combustible gases or vapors
Chemical Incompatibilities, Materials
with: Carbides, Lithium Silicide, Fluorine. Release of dangerous gases or vapors with: Aluminum, Hydrides,
Incompatible
Formaldehyde, Metals, Solutions of Alkaline Hydroxides, Sulfides. Risk of explosion with: Alkali metals,
Concentrated Sulfuric Acid. Incompatible with metals, metal alloys, releases Hydrogen.
Dangerous polymerization If mixed with Aldehydes (generation of heat and pressure).
Hazardous decomposition products Chlorine and Hydrogen.
