SECTION 5: Firefighting measures
Extinguishing agents: In general dry chemical powders and carbon dioxide (CO2).
Special procedures for fighting fire: Do not use water where there is acid, the reaction
It is exothermic (a large amount of heat is released) and violent (splashes). Use water only in
form of fog and to cool the environment. Approach the fire in the direction of the wind. Evacuate
the area by placing personnel against the direction of the wind.
Personal protective equipment for fighting fire: Use breathing equipment
autonomous with a full face mask, positive pressure and on demand or with another system of
positive pressure or full face mask with filter, this in low concentrations. The
clothing can be a suit, boots, gloves and splash safety glasses or clothing
Fully encapsulated acid resistant.
SECTION 6: Measures to be taken in case of accidental spill
Emergency measures to take if there is a material spill: Contain the acid spill or leak
with dikes using dry sand, earth or concrete. Ventilate the area. Isolate the risk sector and
evacuate the area. If it occurs on the road, move the vehicle off the road if possible and
Park in a place where there is less risk to third parties. Request specialized help if necessary
necessary. Recover the liquid if possible and neutralize the residue with fine limestone or with
baking soda.
Personal protective equipment to attack the emergency: Encapsulated suit resistant to acid and
respiratory protection, use Self-Contained Breathing Equipment.

Precautions to take to avoid damage to the environment: Do not dispose directly into drains or courses.
of water. Do not allow acid to enter sewers and water sources.
Cleaning methods: Absorb with an inert material or product such as dry sand.
Collect waste using a safe alternative. Deposit in containers marked for your
subsequent recovery or treatment as chemical waste. Neutralize with lime or baking soda
sodium.
Waste disposal method: Dilute in water in a ratio of about 1:5 and then
neutralize to a pH 6-8, slowly adding a Sodium Hydroxide solution to the acid. The
resulting saline solution, if applicable, diluted with more water in a 1:10 or other ratio
that is necessary. The disposal of the waste generated must be carried out respecting the
limiting conditions established by the corresponding competent authority.
SECTION 7: Handling and storage
Technical recommendations: Store tightly closed. Dry. In a well ventilated place. At temperature
ambient (recommended between 15 and 25° C).
Precautions to take: Store away from incompatible conditions and products. Protect
against physical damage to containers, tanks, ducts, valves and other accessories involved.
Maintain preset levels of tank fills, pressure and working temperature.
Consider spill containment systems, leaks under ponds. Have the packaging
closed and properly labeled.
Specific safe handling recommendations: Storage conditions: Area
storage of chemical products with contact risk. Storage in cellar, cabins
or ponds resistant to corrosive substances. Cool and cold place, minimum humidity and with good
ventilation. Risk signs for containers, tanks, pipes and storage areas.
Restricted access to storage areas.
Recommended packaging: Glass jars of up to 5 liters, drums of
reinforced plastic up to 35 liters, steel drums up to 200 liters
, shaped ponds
cylindrical or hexahedral with cut edges with a maximum capacity of 3000 liters. Cisterns or
stainless steel ponds.
SECTION 8: Exposure Controls/Personal Protection
Measures to reduce the possibility of exposure: Work in places with good ventilation. To the
laboratory users, use existing hoods. Respect prohibitions on not smoking, eating
and drinking in the workplace. When diluting, always add the acid to the water, never the opposite. No
use your mouth to pipette. Use personal protection elements.

SECTION 10: Stability and reactivity
Stability: Stable only under normal conditions.
Conditions to avoid: Temperatures, decomposes at 338°C
Incompatibility (materials to avoid): Acrylonitrile, alkaline solutions, carbides,
chlorates, fulminates, nitrates, perchlorates, permanganates, etc. With metals you can produce gas
hydrogen which is flammable. Acetaldehyde can violently polymerize with generation of
heat. It reacts violently with water, generating heat.
Hazardous decomposition products: Sulfurous and sulfuric anhydrides and hydrogen.
Hazardous combustion products: Toxic and corrosive gases may be released in
fires.
Hazardous polymerization: Not applicable.
SECTION 11: Toxicological information
Short-term toxicity: No special observations.
Long-term toxicity: No special observations.
Local effects: Severe burns after contact with skin with ulcerations. Corneal injuries.
After ingestion, severe pain, nausea, vomiting and diarrhea occur.
Allergen sensitization: Not available.
