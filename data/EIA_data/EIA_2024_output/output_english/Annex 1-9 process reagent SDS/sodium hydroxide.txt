EXPECTED ACUTE EFFECTS:
Inhalation (Breathing): Respiratory System Effects: Exposure to airborne material may
cause irritation, redness of the upper and lower respiratory tract, cough, laryngospasm and edema,
breathing difficulties, bronchoconstriction and possible pulmonary edema. It can cause severe scarring and
permanent. Pulmonary edema may develop several hours after severe, acute exposure.
Vacuuming this material can cause the same problems.
Skin: Corrosion of the skin. Skin exposure may cause redness, itching, irritation, swelling,
burns (first, second or third degree), liquefaction of the skin and damage to underlying tissues (wounds
deep and painful).
Eyes: Serious damage to eyes. Eye exposures may cause burns to the eyelids,
conjunctivitis, corneal edema, corneal burn, corneal perforation, damage to the contents of the eye, defects
permanent vision and blindness and/or loss of the eye.
Ingestion (Swallowing): Gastrointestinal effects: Exposure through ingestion may cause irritation,
inflammation and perforation of the upper gastrointestinal tissues. Permanent scarring may occur.
EXPECTED DELAYED EFFECTS:
Skin: repeated and prolonged contact with the skin can cause chronic dermatitis
PROTECTION OF THOSE PROVIDING FIRST AID:
Protect yourself by avoiding contact with this material. Avoid contact with skin and eyes. Do not breathe the fog,
vapors or sprays. Do not ingest. Use personal protective equipment. At a minimum, when treating staff,
sufficient eq must be used
Personal protective equipment to prevent the transmission of pathogens by
blood. See section 8 for specific personal protective equipment recommendations.
Interaction with Other Chemicals That Enhance Toxicity: None known.
MEDICAL CONDITIONS AGGRAVATED BY EXPOSURE:
It can aggravate pre-existing conditions, such as eye disorders that decrease the production of
tears or that reduce the integrity of the eye; skin disorders that compromise the integrity of the skin; and
respiratory diseases such as asthma and other respiratory disorders.
SPECIAL NOTES FOR A TREATING PHYSICIAN:
Medical observation and evaluation is recommended in all cases of ingestion and ocular exposure, as well as
inhalation and symptomatic skin exposure. In cases of symptomatic ingestion, do not administer intravenous fluids.
oral and consider exploration by endoscopy, radiography or computed tomography (CT). It is possible that
produce esophageal perforation, airway compromise, hypotension and shock. In case of exposure
prolonged and significant, consider late injuries to exposed tissues. There is no antidote. The treatment
consists of palliative care. Follow normal parameters for airway, breathing, and circulation.
Surgical intervention may be required.
