The goal of ACGIH (and other Agencies) is to recommend TLVs (or their equivalent) for all substances for which there are
evidence of health effects at workplace air concentrations.
Until now, no TLV has been established, although this material can produce adverse health effects (as evidenced in
animal experiments or clinical experience). Airborne concentrations should be kept as low as practically
possible and occupational exposure should be kept to a minimum.
Sensory irritants are chemicals that produce temporary and undesirable side effects in the eyes, nose or throat. Historically
Occupational exposure standards for these irritants have been based on observation of workers' responses to various
concentrations in the air. Current expectations require that almost every individual be protected against even the slightest sensory irritation and
Exposure standards are established using uncertainty or safety factors of 5 to 10 or more. Sometimes effect levels do not
observable in animals (non-observable-animal-effect-levels (NOEL)) are used to determine these limits when results in humans do not
are available. An additional method, typically used by the TLV committee (USA) in determining respiratory standards for this group
of chemicals, has been to assign limit values (TLV C) to rapidly acting irritants and assign short-term exposure limits (TLV STELs)
when the weight of evidence of irritation, bioaccumulation and other factors combine to warrant such a limit. In contrast to the Commission
MAK (Germany) uses a five-category system based on odor intensity, local irritation, and elimination half-life. However, this system
is being replaced to be consistent with the European Union (EU) Scientific Committee for Occupational Exposure Limits (SCOEL); this is
more closely related to that of the United States. OSHA (USA) concluded that exposure to sensory irritants can cause:

the risk. Well-designed engineering controls can be very effective in protecting

worker interactions.

Process controls that involve changing the way a work activity or process is performed.
