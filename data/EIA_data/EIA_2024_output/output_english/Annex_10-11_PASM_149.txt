1 INTRODUCTION
The Mining Development Project of Clay Extraction for the Production of Earth Concentrate
Raras, hereinafter, without distinction, the “Project” corresponds to the set of parts, works and actions
that will allow the construction, operation and closure of a mining site for extraction and processing
of clays, a material present in the area, which, due to natural effects, is highly
weathered, with the presence of rare earths in clay minerals. The Project will be developed
mainly in the commune of Penco, Province of Co
ncepción, Biobío Region, without prejudice to the
of the works are located in the commune of Concepción, on the border with the Commune of Penco.
The Project considers the following facilities: one (1) Processing Plant, three (3) storage areas
clay extraction (ZE Victoria Norte, ZE Luna, ZE Maite), one (1) disposal area for ballast and clays
processed (ZD Neptuno), one (1) vegetal soil collection area (ZAV), a processing plant
clays (the “Plant”), a stockpile for the temporary collection of material prior to processing, a line
electricity and its connection to the existing line and the construction of internal roads to access the different
areas. The Processing Plant will have a capacity to process up to 320 tons/hour of
material to produce Rare Earth concentrate. In addition, the modification of a section is considered
existing approximately 1.22 kilometers of the Hualpén – San Fernando pipeline, owned by
ENAP, reason for the exploitation of the Maite ZE. The Project in turn includes the implementation of works
temporary corresponding to work facilities for the construction and authorization of the works of the
Project.
This document addresses the sectoral environmental permit of article 149 (PAS 149) of the Regulation
of the Environmental Impact Assessment System (SEIA) modified through Supreme Decree (D.S.) No.
40/2012 of the Ministry of the Environment (MMA), regarding the permit for cutting plantations in
lands of preferably forestry suitability necessary for the execution of any project or
activity of those indicated in article 3 of the SEIA Regulation, with the exception of the projects to which
literal m.1 refers, will be that established in article 21 of Decree Law (D.L.) No. 701 of 1974 of the
Ministry of Agriculture (MINAGRI), which establishes the legal regime for forest lands or preferably
suitable for afforestation, and establishes promotion regulations on the matter, the text of which was replaced
by D.L. No. 2,565 of 1979 of MINAGRI, which replaces D.L. No. 701 of 1974, which subjects lands
forestry to the provisions it indicates.
The Mining Development Project for Clay Extraction for the Production of Rare Earth Concentrate
is located in the commune of Penco, Province of Concepción, Biobío Region, however, part of
The works are located in the commune of Concepción, on the border with the commune of Penco. This Project
consists of the construction and operation of a mining site, along with a processing plant, the
which has a capacity to process up to 320 tons/hour of material and produce approximately
3,100 tons per year of Rare Earth concentrate.

b) Description of the works associated with the intervention
This permit is part of the “Clay Extraction Mining Development Project for
Production of Rare Earth Concentrate”, which corresponds to a set of works and activities that
will allow the construction and operation of a mining site and a clay processing plant,
material present in the area, which due to natural effects is highly weathered, with
presence of lanthanides adsorbed on clay minerals.
Lanthanides are 15 elements: lanthanum (La), cerium (Ce), praseodymium (Pr), neodymium (Nd), promethium (Pm),
samarium (Sm), europium (Eu), gadolinium (Gd), terbium (Tb), dysprosium (Dy), holmium (Ho), erbium (Er), thulium (Tm),
ytterbium (Yb) and lutetium (Lu). The elements named above along with yttrium (Y) and scandium (Sc)
They are known as Rare Earths.
Of the 17 elements mentioned above, 15 of them are found in this project.
present in the material, which are: lanthanum (La), cerium (Ce), praseodymium (Pr), neodymium (Nd), samarium
(Sm), europium (Eu), gadolinium (Gd), terbium (Tb), dysprosium (Dy), holmium (Ho), erbium (Er), thulium (Tm), ytterbium
(Yb), lutetium (Lu) and yttrium (Y).
The objective of the Project is to build and operate a mining site and a concentrate production plant
of rare earths with a capacity to process up to 320 tons/hour of clays and produce in the order
of 3,100 tons per year of Rare Earth concentrate.
For this, the Project considers three (3) clay extraction zones (ZE Victoria Norte
, ZE Moon, ZE
Maite), one (1) disposal area for waste and processed clays (ZD Neptuno), one (1)
topsoil stockpile (ZAV), a clay processing plant (the Plant) and a stockpile for the
temporary stockpile of material prior to processing, an electrical line and its connection to an existing line and
the construction of internal roads to access the different areas. Furthermore, it is considered the
construction of a by-pass of the Hualpén – San Fernando oil pipeline, property of ENAP, reason for the
exploitation of the Maite ZE. The project in turn includes the implementation of temporary works
corresponding to work facilities, for the construction and authorization of the Project works.

When the cutting areas are adjacent to road-type works, when intercepting a cause, a
execute works whose objective is not to interfere with the natural course of waters, preserving the network of
natural drainage. The function of the hydraulic works associated with road crossings is to preserve the
continuity of the flow from the generated runoff, which will be intercepted by the
projected road infrastructure.
The stream crossings will be materialized in two types:
- Sewers: They correspond to the location of one or more galvanized steel pipes
corrugated (or High Density Polyethylene technically similar to corrugated steel) below
the road surface to be enabled. These are located at a defined depth to
not be affected by the live loads of the trucks that will transit through the project area. The
entrance to the sewer will be lined with poor concrete structures in order to
conduct water into the tube(s) and serve as structural support.
- Boxes: They correspond to reinforced concrete works with a rectangular section, and can be
prefabricated elements are used as long as they comply with the dimensions associated with the flow
defined carrying. These works will be installed below the road, at a
defined depth so as not to be affected by the live loads of the trucks that will transit through
the project area. The entrance to the caisson is lined with a concrete structure
reinforced, as well as wing walls at both ends, in order to improve driving
of water towards the box, reduce erosion on the sides of the channel and reduce the energy of
entry of water to the work.

forests identified in the area of influence, that is, 101.72 hectares of the Oak forest type will be reforested.
Raulí-Coigüe and 1.03 ha of the Sclerophil forest type, equivalent to 99% and 1% respectively. They are considered

erosive.

4 OBJECTIVES OF THE SHORT
This permit is part of the “Clay Extraction Mining Development Project for
Production of Rare Earth Concentrate”, which corresponds to a set of works and activities that
will allow the construction and operation of a mining site and a clay processing plant,
material present in the area, which due to natural effects is highly weathered, with
presence of lanthanides adsorbed on clay minerals.
Lanthanides are 15 elements: lanthanum (La), cerium (Ce), praseodymium (Pr), neodymium (Nd), promethium
(Pm), samarium (Sm), europium (Eu), gadolinium (Gd), terbium (Tb), dysprosium (Dy), holmium (Ho), erbium (Er), thulium
(Tm), ytterbium (Yb) and lutetium (Lu). The elements named above along with yttrium (Y) and
Scandium (Sc) are known as Rare Earths.

topsoil stockpile (ZAV), a clay processing plant (the Plant) and a stockpile for the
temporary stockpile of material prior to processing, an electrical line and its connection to an existing line
and the construction of internal roads to access the different areas. Furthermore, it is considered the
construction of a by-pass of the Hualpén – San Fernando oil pipeline, property of ENAP, reason for the
exploitation of the Maite ZE. The project in turn includes the implementation of temporary works
corresponding to work facilities, for the construction and authorization of the Project works.
The construction and implementation of the parts, works and actions of the Project during the phase of
construction and operation, involves the intervention of surfaces for the leveling and enabling of
lands, with a series of activities, among which we can mention earthworks
and escarpments, which involve cutting, clearing or loss of vegetation activities.
During the construction phase, the Victoria North extraction area will be enabled,
Luna extraction area, Neptuno disposal area, plant collection area, temporary Stockpile and the
roads for access to each area. In addition, an electrical transmission line will be implemented and the
construction of the processing plant.
In the case of roads, the improvement of existing roads and construction of new ones is contemplated.
Projected internal roads. The preparation of roads consists of clearing vegetation,
leveling, and stabilization with gravel. Gravel for road stabilization will eventually come
by material extracted from the extraction areas.
The road cutting material that will not be used in works associated with the project will be sent for
its disposition to the disposition zone (ZD) Neptune.
For its part, during the operation phase, the ZE Maite zone will be enabled and the
modification of the Hualpén – San Fernando pipeline.
Given the temporary or permanent condition of the Project, some works in the construction phase
and operation present overlaps in their location areas, or are contained
within others. During the construction phase, an area of approximately 2.7 hectares will be equipped
the installation of tasks, temporary work, which will be located in the location area of
the processing plant. This surface is not considered in the intervention, since it corresponds to the
surface area associated with the construction of the Processing Plant.

individuals to local environmental conditions. For this, a decreasing program of
irrigation in volume and frequency approximately during the following three (3) years, watering at the end
of the process only in case of risks of mortality and state of the plants and finally eliminating it
completely. This program will be
subject to evaluation and monitoring of the climate situation
prevailing within the area where the reforestation will be carried out. The objective of an irrigation system
decreasing is to help the establishment of plants, without producing a dependency effect on them.
