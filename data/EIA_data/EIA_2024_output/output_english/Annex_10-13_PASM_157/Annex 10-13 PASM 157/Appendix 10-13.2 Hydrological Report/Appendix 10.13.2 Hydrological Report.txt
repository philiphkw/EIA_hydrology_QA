1. General background
This study is carried out within the framework of the project called Development Project
Clay Extraction Miner for the Production of Rare Earths of the REE company
UNO SpA, hereinafter The Project, which consists of the construction and operation of
a mining site together with a weathered material processing plant
granitic origin with presence of Lanthanides, widely known as Tierras
Rare. For the development of this study, the hydrological characterization is considered
associated with the area of influence of the Project, having background information
public and bibliographical.
It should be noted that the project will not require the exploitation of natural water resources.
(both underground and surface) of some course, body or reservoir adjacent to
the project works for execution
on of their industrial processes, counting these
activities with external sources of use. In fact, the only interaction
between the project and the superficial courses of the area of influence lies in the
construction of a series of underground, surface or aerial crossings
linked to the implementation of conduction pipelines and enabling of routes
transportation.
To study the hydrological conditions of the project area, it is taken into account
consideration of the sources of information available from the General Directorate of Water
(DGA) in terms of hydrography, rainfall and analysis methodologies.
1.1. Study Area
The area in which the hydrological study is carried out corresponds to the future location
of the Project which is located east of the city of Penco, Biobío Region. Your

2.4.1.2. Homogeneity Verification
The data obtained from rainfall stations must be reviewed and
compared to each other, in order to verify their consistency and avoid errors that
may contain the product of measurement or transcription omissions. To verify the
homogeneity between stations, a consistency analysis is carried out using the
double cumulative curves method.
The double accumulated curves method consists of graphing the accumulated precipitation
of the analysis station versus the accumulated value of a standard station, built
with the average of neighboring stations. This method is based on the hypothesis that,
If the area is pluviometrically homogeneous, the precipitation between the
analysis and the standard station, must be statistically proportional. The station
analyzed is considered homogeneous if the coefficient of determination (R2) results in a
value greater than 0.7 (Stowhas B, 2016).

2.5.3.3. DGA-AC method
The DGA-AC method for pluvial floods corresponds to a regional flood analysis
of pluvial origin, based on series of annual maximums generated from the
information on maximum average daily and maximum instantaneous flows of the period
rainfall, of 234 fluviometric control stations.
This method covers stations from the regions of Atacama to Araucanía, it is valid
for rainwater or pluvial basins without fluviometric information and with areas
between 20 and 10,000 km2. Its use is restricted to return periods
less than 100 years old.
Because the basins in this study do not exceed 2 km2 in area, it is
They include the results of this methodology for comparison purposes only.
The method basically consists of determining a frequency curve for the flow
maximum instantaneous of the basin. For this, the homogeneous area must be defined to which
the basin under study belongs, then determine the frequency curve of the basin
for the maximum average daily flow variable. Finally, this curve becomes
maximum instantaneous flow rates through the determination of a conversion factor
appropriate.

1. General background
The Mining Development Project for the Extraction of Clays for the Production of
Rare Earth Concentrate, administratively located in the Commune of Penco and
Concepción, Province of Concepción, Biobío Region, consists of the construction and
operation of a mining site together with a material processing plant
weathered granitic, with presence of clays. The regolith, hereinafter called
“Clays to be processed” are formed as a result of high weathering of rocks.
and contains lanthanides adsorbed on clay minerals. Lanthanides, or Earths
Rare, there are 15 elements whose atomic number increases from Lanthanum (57) to Lutetium
(71). Due to their similar chemical behavior, yttrium (Y) is added to them. From now on they will be
called “rare earths”.
The plant has a capacity to process up to 320 tons/hour of material and
produce approximately 3,100 tons per year of Rare Earth concentrate.
In addition to the above, the Project considers three (3) clay extraction zones,
where on the south side of one of them (ZE Maite) an oil pipeline is planned
ENAP underground. The main objective of the development of this study is
quantify runoff at 2 new crossing points of natural channels with the
pipeline, to then be able to carry out hydraulic modeling and evaluate scour
general.
To study the hydrological conditions of the project area, it is taken into account
consideration of the sources of information available from the General Directorate of Water
(DGA) in terms of hydrography, rainfall and analysis methodologies.

2.4.1.2. Homogeneity Verification
The data obtained from rainfall stations must be reviewed and
compared to each other, in order to verify their consistency and avoid errors that
may contain the product of measurement or transcription omissions. To verify the
homogeneity between stations, a consistency analysis is carried out using the
double cumulative curves method.
The double accumulated curves method consists of graphing the accumulated precipitation
of the analysis station versus the accumulated value of a standard station, built
with the average of neighboring stations. This method is based on the hypothesis that,
If the area is pluviometrically homogeneous, the precipitation between the
analysis and the standard station, must be statistically proportional. The station
analyzed is considered homogeneous if the coefficient of determination (R2) results in a
value greater than 0.7 (Stowhas B, 2016).
