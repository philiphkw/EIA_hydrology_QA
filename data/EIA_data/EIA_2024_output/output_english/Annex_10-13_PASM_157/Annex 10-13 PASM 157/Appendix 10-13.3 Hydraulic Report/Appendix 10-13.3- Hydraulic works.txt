1. General background
This study is carried out within the framework of the project called Development Project
Clay Extraction Miner for the Production of Rare Earth Concentrate
the company REE UNO SpA, hereinafter The Project, which consists of the construction and
operation of a mining site together with a material processing plant
weathered of granitic origin with the presence of Lanthanides, widely known
like Rare Earths. For the development of this study, the characterization is considered
hydrology associated with the area of influence of the Project, having for this purpose
public and bibliographic background.
It should be noted that the project will not require the exploitation of natural water resources.
(both underground and surface) of some course, body or reservoir adjacent to
the project works for the execution of its industrial processes, counting said
activities with external sources of use. In fact, the only interaction
between the project and the superficial courses of the area of influence lies in the
construction of a series of underground, surface or aerial crossings
linked to the implementation of conduction pipelines and enabling of routes
transportation.
The hydraulic works, whose sizing is considered in this report are the
following:
• Contour channels in the Maite, Luna and Victoria Norte Extraction Zones,
Neptune Disposal Zone and Temporary Plant Material Collection Zone
(ZAV) of the Project, which will be presented within PAS 157.
• Boxes and culverts for crossing streams on internal roads, works
that will be presented within PAS 156.
The sizing of the works was carried out based on the hydrology presented as
appendix 10-13.2A of this EAI, for PAS 156 and 157. The dimensioning of the
Other hydraulic works of the project are not part of the scope of this report.
The objective of this appendix is to establish the design and parameters used for this
and for the verification of the hydraulic works implemented in the project, as well as
also serve as technical support for PAS 156 and 157.

2.6. Channel verification
The verification flows (for a return period of 150 years) for the areas of
Neptuno disposition and Acopio Vegetal ZAV were obtained from a
linear interpolation between the flows for 100 and 200 years, given that they were estimated
from the Rational Method. As the design flow rates of the contour channels
in the extraction areas they come from the internal evacuation channels, and these were
estimated using a methodology of exceedance probabilities, is used as
amplification factor the ratio between the verification and design flow rates of the
plant disposal and storage areas. This factor takes a value of 1.055 and is applied to
the design flows of each contour channel in extraction areas to be able to
perform the verification analogous to the flow of 150 years.
With this flow, the hydraulic head generated in the channel is calculated and it is verified that this
height does not exceed the proposed design in each of the extraction areas,
plant disposition and collection. This verification is tabulated in Annex 10-13.3
– Design of canals and culverts.

Contour channels are those located on the edges of the Zones
of Plant Extraction, Disposal and Collection. These have contributions from the
runoff from the surrounding hills and streams, in addition to contributions from the canals
evacuators (in the case of Extraction areas).

ratio 1:1 (45°), covered with lean concrete (or technically similar solution) and
stones of irregular shape, in order to reduce the energy of the flow. In Figure 7,

1.1. General background
The Mining Development Project for Clay Extraction for the Production of Concentrate
Rare Earths, administratively located in the Commune of Penco and Concepción, Province of
Concepción, Biobío Region, consists of the construction and operation of a mining site together
with a processing plant for weathered granitic material, with the presence of clays.
Regolith, hereinafter called “Clays to be processed”, is formed as a result of a high
weathering of rocks and contains lanthanides adsorbed on clay minerals. The
Lanthanides, or Rare Earths, are 15 elements whose atomic number increases from Lanthanum
(57) to lutetium (71). Due to their similar chemical behavior, yttrium (Y) is added to them. From now on
They will be called “rare earths”.
The plant has a capacity to process up to 320 tons/hour of material and produce
approximately 3,100 tons per year of Rare Earth concentrate.
In addition to the above, the Project considers three (3) clay extraction zones, where in the
south side of one of them (ZE Maite) the modification of an underground oil pipeline is planned
from ENAP. The main objective of the development of this study is to evaluate the general undermining
in these 2 new crossing points of natural channels with the pipeline through
2-dimensional hydraulic modeling with the HEC-RAS software, in order to determine if the
buried pipe is affected by runoff (See Appendix 10.13.2B – Study
Hydrological). 2 scenarios will be evaluated, situation without project (unaltered stream) and with
project (ravine being intervened as an Extraction area).

1.5.5. Edge Conditions
HEC-RAS Software provides a wide range of boundary conditions to be applied to the
model. For this case, two types of boundary conditions were considered: Flow hydrograph
and “Normal Depth”.
To define the inflow in the model, the boundary conditions were considered to be
previously calculated hydrographs. These conditions will be located in the headers of the
basins, in order to reflect the flow entering the modeling area. Furthermore, it is considered
the energy line as the slope of the terrain, which is used to calculate a depth
normal from the given flow rate and cross section data along
of the boundary condition line for each calculation time step. Next, it
calculates a flow distribution in the cross section, based on the water surface at
normal depth and flow rate in the cross section below the condition line
2D outline.
To define the flow output of the model, the normal depth condition is used.
When using this boundary condition, a friction slope must be entered
for
that area. The friction slope can be based on the slope of the terrain in the vicinity
of the boundary condition line of the 2D flow area. Just like the boundary condition
of the Hydrograph, the normal depth condition is calculated based on individual cells.
Thus, the zones at the edge of the modeling are defined as flow outlets of the system, of
so that water does not artificially accumulate within the flood area and can occupy
all the space you have available, as you would in reality.
