1 INTRODUCTION
The Mining Development Project of Clay Extraction for the Production of Earth Concentrate
Raras corresponds to the set of works and activities that will allow the construction and operation of a
mining site and a clay processing plant, a material present in the area, which due to the effects
natural is highly weathered, with the presence of lanthanides adsorbed in the minerals
of clay. Lanthanides are 15 elements: lanthanum (La), cerium (Ce), praseodymium (Pr), neodymium (Nd),
promethium (Pm), samarium (Sm), europium (Eu), gadolinium (Gd), terbium (Tb), dysprosium (Dy), holmium (Ho), erbium
(Er), thulium (Tm), ytterbium (Yb) and lutetium (Lu). The elements named above along with yttrium (Y) and
scandium (Sc) are known as Rare Earths.
The processing plant has a capacity to process up to 320 tons/hour of clays and
produce Rare Earth concentrate.
In addition to the above, the Project considers three (3) clay extraction zones (ZE Victoria Norte, ZE
Luna, ZE Maite), one (1) disposal area for waste and washed clays (ZD Neptuno), one (1) disposal area
collection of topsoil (ZAV), an electrical line and
its connection to the existing line and the construction of
Internal roads to access the different areas. In addition, the implementation of works is considered
temporary works corresponding to work facilities, for the construction and authorization of the works of the
Project.
Regarding Sectoral Environmental Permit No. 137, the Project, given its characteristics, requires the
Permission for the approval of the Closure Plan of a Mining Site. In this context, Article 137 of the
Regulation of the Environmental Impact Assessment System (RSEIA) D.S. No. 40/2012, MMA, states:
Article 137.- Permission for the approval of the closure plan of a mining site.
The permit for the execution of the closure plan of a mining site will be as established in article 6 of
Law 20,551, on the Closure of Mines and Mining Facilities.
The requirements for its granting consist of ensuring the physical and chemical stability of the work.
the mining extractive industry, in order to provide due protection to the life and health of people
and environment.
The technical and formal contents that must be presented to prove compliance are the
following:

i. Coastal Batholith
It corresponds to a plutonic complex with lithologies between tonalite and granodiorite, with limited areas of

It crops out in a NNE-SSW strip to the east of the Accretionary Metamorphic Complex.
The intrusive units of the Coastal Batholith instruct the Eastern Series during the Upper Paleozoic
of the Metamorphic Complex, generating a regional contact metamorphism type Buchan (low pressure

The rocks of the Costeo Batholith, in the areas near the Project, underlie the sedimentary formations

ii. Metamorphic Complex
The Paleozoic Metamorphic Basement of the Coastal Mountain Range, which consists of a strip of about 800
km long and 30 km wide on average Aguirre (1972) divides the strip into two main units:
Oriental Series and Western Series, which differ from each other due to their dominant lithology, present structures and
metamorphic grade that affects them. The metamorphic units of the complex are interpreted as a
paired metamorphic belt consisting of a series of low P/T to the east and another of high P/T to the west
(Aguirre et al.). 197; Hervé, 1988;). Currently, both series are interpreted as results of a
frontal and basal accretion, respectively, in an active continental margin context (Willner et al.
2005; Glodny et al., 2005, Glodny et al., 2008).
The Oriental Series is the dominant one in the study area, it is mainly composed of a
alternation of metapelites and metarenites of turbidite origin (Hervé et al., 2007, 2013), which were
deposited in a deep stable continental margin and accreted to the continent upon restarting the
subduction during the Ordovician (Glodny et al., 2005). The series is subjected, from the west, to an accretion
frontal, which generates foliation in the metasediments and is responsible for a regional metamorphism of the
Barrovian type, expressed by pretectonic associations of staurolite, garnet and biotite (Vásquez,
2001).

2.3.9.3 Risks of mass removal
Localized mass removal deposits limited to areas were identified in the Project area.
with absence of vegetation and/or where highly weathered hillside or slope is exposed, which favors
the detachment and removal of the surface layer; These localized mass movements are
associated with anthropogenic slopes and areas of anthropogenic terraces present in the place.
The risk of mass removal is associated with a process of slow or rapid mobilization of a certain
volume of soil, rock or both, in various proportions, and generated by various factors (Hauser,
1993). These movements have a descending character since they are fundamentally controlled by
gravity (Cruden, 1991).
The triggering factors of a removal process are related to the susceptibility that
presents a slope, depending on different interactions, either in the interaction of agents
conditioning factors such as: geology, geotechnics, geomorphology, hydrology and hydrogeology, vegetation, climate
and anthropogenic activity; and/or depending on triggering agents such as precipitation, earthquakes
and other less frequent factors such as volcanic activity, snow melting,
anthropic, etc. (Lara et al, 2008).
