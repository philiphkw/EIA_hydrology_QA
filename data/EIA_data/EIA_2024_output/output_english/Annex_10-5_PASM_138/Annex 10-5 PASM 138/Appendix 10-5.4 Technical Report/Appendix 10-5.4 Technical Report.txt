1. INTRODUCTION
The main objective of this project is to design the sewer networks and the
activated sludge type wastewater treatment in extended aeration mode,
intended to satisfy the health needs of 373 workers during the
construction phase of the “Clay Extraction Mining Development Project for
Production of Rare Earth Concentrate”. These facilities are located in the
commune of Penco, province of Concepción, Biobío region.
In addition to providing a comprehensive sanitary solution, the use of effluent is contemplated
treated for the wetting of roads within the same property, which includes a
emergency connection through a drainage type infiltration system designed to
manage 50% of the daily effluent generated.
It is essential to highlight that the land where this project will be carried out lacks the
feasibility of connecting to a public sewer system, which has motivated the
choosing this solution as the most appropriate.

waters comparable to domestic, which purifies up to 90% of the materials

After the wastewater passes through the Decanter, it enters the Biological Reactor.

digest the biodegradable organic matter present in the water, thus reducing
considerably the Biochemical Oxygen Demand (BOD).

obtain bubbles of a suitable size to optimize oxygen transfer
and achieve an adequate biological oxidation process.
This design allows efficient, safe operation without risk of obstruction.

Stage IV: Sludge digester (aerobic)
The main objective of the sludge digester is to reduce the amount of matter
organic present in the sludge, which helps minimize the volume of sludge and
convert them into a more stable product and less prone to rot and
release of unpleasant odors.
The main advantages of aerobic digestion are: lower
BOD concentrations in the supernatant liquid: the initial investment is usually
minor; A biologically stable final product is obtained, without odors. The disadvantages
can be important, among them: a higher energy cost associated with the supply
of the necessary oxygen; the characteristics of the digested sludge are not the best, for
apply mechanical dehydration. It should also be remembered that digestion
Anaerobic is also very delicate in terms of operation.
Stage V: Disinfection
After the water has been treated, it goes through a disinfection stage using
a Chlorination and Dechlorination system to ensure the destruction of Coliforms
and other pathogenic microorganisms.

through openings, or under suspended partitions that act as skimmers.

the clarifier, which must be low enough to allow sedimentation
efficient, and high enough to maintain an adequate amount of solids from
return and provide a thickened return concentration.

Anaerobic is also very delicate in terms of operation.

5.5. Management, quantity generated, frequency and place of final removal of sludge
According to the installation, maintenance and operation manual of the treatment plant,
after properly balancing the variable capacities of the plant, such as
mixing and aeration, with operating time according to the organic load,
The normal operation of the wastewater treatment plant begins.

5.5. Management, quantity generated, frequency and place of final removal of sludge
According to the installation, maintenance and operation manual of the treatment plant,
after properly balancing the variable capacities of the plant, such as
mixing and aeration, with operating time according to the organic load,
The normal operation of the wastewater treatment plant begins.
Good plant management consists of maintaining an optimal level of the sludge blanket in
the clarifier, which must be low enough to allow sedimentation
efficient, and high enough to maintain an adequate amount of solids from
return and provide a thickened return concentration.
The extraction of sludge, according to the PTAS operation and maintenance manual, is
must be done by authorized pit cleaning truck every 6 months. Where the
crust and the solids decanted to the bottom of the compartment are extracted (It is extracted between
30% and 50% of the compartment).
The final destination of the sludge will be in Authorized treatment plants in the region.
The type of sludge generated is unstabilized.

mechanics.

Pipe transport
The transportation of material includes the transfer of all pipes from
suppliers' warehouse to the work.
The transportation of the pipes must be carried out following the stipulations that at the
respect to the manufacturer.
The loading and transportation procedure must be planned in order to avoid
damage to pipes caused by impacts during movement or
due to excessive efforts produced by the elements used.
The collection of pipes in the means of transport must respect the following
conditions:
• The maximum storage height should not exceed 2 m.
• The lower layer of tubes will be supported on transverse planks of a thickness
minimum of 25 mm placed 1 m from the ends.
• A similar support will be arranged between layers of tubes.
• The lateral end tubes must be secured with plugs or wedges.
that prevent displacement.
After unloading and before the tubes are covered in the pile
collection, a review will be carried out to detect those that present breaks,
jumps or any other fault, rejecting those that are in
these conditions, and must be removed from the site and stored in
separate place.
With PVC pipes (if applicable) the following must be taken into account:
considerations:
• Both the interior and sealing surfaces of the pipe and fittings
union, they must be kept free of dust and foreign matter.
• The stack of pipes should be stored on flat surfaces that
provide uniform support. Storing pipes should be avoided
outside and exposed to the sun.
• In any case when it cannot be avoided, the pipes must be
covered by canvas and other opaque material, avoiding plastic covers
transparent. Under cover, adequate
air circulation.
• Materials must be handled according to the recommendations
given by the manufacturer to prevent damage. Pipe and fittings
union should not be thrown, dropped or dragged on the ground.

recommendations:

possible or replaced.

float.

suitable.
