1. INTRODUCTION
Due to the geographical characteristics of the Project Area of Influence and the low accessibility of certain
sections as a result of the slope and the dense vegetation cover, along with the inspection, a
georeferenced photographic record of the areas that had strong access restrictions, which did not
allowed a systematic archaeological study through transects.
In general terms, the Area of influence of the Project presents slopes that fluctuate from low (0-
2°) to very steep (about 35°) and to moderately steep (20-30°) (Chapter 3.5. Baseline
Geomorphology of the Project).
Various inactive streams are observed in the Project areas, as well as a river course that borders the
Northern sector of the works (Estero Penco), which gives rise to the growth of dense plant formations in
around its channel. Likewise, the presence of properties with pine and eucalyptus forest plantations is observed.
in a large part of the Project area, in various stages of growth (from saplings to plantations
mature), associated with which the presence of wild forest of exotic species (Pine and
Eucalyptus) and the growth of shrub species and formations of the type of cover scrub that varies
from open to dense.
In general terms, the presence of forest plantations and the growth of bush vegetation, quila
and/or blackberry associated with them, along with the presence of steep slopes and eroded soils
product of forestry activity, constituted access limitations that strongly conditioned the
archaeological inspection coverage.
In order to account for the geographical conditions of the non-prospectable sections (either due to slope
or due to the presence of dense vegetation formations), a total of 172 points of
control in different non-accessible sections of the Project areas, with UTM coordinates and photographs
panoramic views, to which a code was assigned (PCA for control points associated with areal works and
PCL for control points associated with linear works), as well as a correlative number depending on their
location with respect to the works, as detailed in the tables and figures presented below.

2.8. ROADS AND LTE INTERVENTION AREA
In the Road Intervention Area (which includes the intervention surfaces associated with the
projected roads and existing roads with expansion), a coverage of
inspection of 66.17% (14.81 ha of the total 22.38 ha), while in the Intervention Area
of the LTE, an opening of 33.5% was reached (0.067 ha of the total 0.2 ha). The sectors do not
prospects correspond mainly to areas with pine forest plantations and/or
Eucalyptus with dense cover vegetation growth inside, as well as on the slopes of
steep slope (over 25°) with the presence of forest plantations (some of them in a state
mature, with risk of logs falling on the accesses due to the slope) and areas with
presence of dense vegetation cover or massive earthworks, close to the Center
of Waste Management (landfill) of Cemarc.

Road Intervention, a total of 44 photographic control points were recorded
georeferenced (called PCL - for their reference to linear works control points -
followed by correlative numbers – from 1 to 44 – depending on their location), which are summarized
in Table 8 and from Figure 8 to Figure 13, whose detailed photographs are presented below.
