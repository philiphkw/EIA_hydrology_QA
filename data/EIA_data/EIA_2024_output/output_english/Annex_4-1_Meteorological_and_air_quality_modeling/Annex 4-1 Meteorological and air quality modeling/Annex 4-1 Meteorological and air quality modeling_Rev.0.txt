1 INTRODUCTION
This document presents the results of the meteorological and dispersion modeling of the
emissions presented in “Annex 1-3 Estimation of atmospheric emissions” of the Development Project
Clay Extraction Miner for Rare Earth Concentrate Production. The modeled scenario
corresponds to the year of highest estimated emissions, and which will be part of the operation phase of the
Project. The Project “Mining Development of Clay Extraction for the Production of Concentrated
Rare Earths” (the Project) has as its objective the construction and operation of a mining site and a
clay processing plant, a material present in the area, which, due to natural effects, is
It is highly weathered, with the presence of rare earths in clay minerals. The Project
will be developed mainly in the commune of Penco, Province of Concepción, Biobío Region, without
prejudice that a minor part of the works are located in the commune of Concepción, on the border with the
Penco Commune.
The modeling considers the guidelines indicated in the Guide for the use of Air Quality Models
in the SEIA, version 2 (2023), for which meteorological modeling and climate modeling are carried out.
emissions dispersion. The contributions in terms of air quality are estimated in receivers of
interest and within the modeling domain defined for a grid of receptors. With the contributions
estimates are evaluated for regulatory compliance, considering the baseline measured in stations
of air quality monitors for the 2020-2022 triennium within the area of influence of the Project.
Additionally, and considering that the area where the Project will be located has a declaration
of saturated zone for the annual standard of MP2.51, the estimated contributions are evaluated considering the
evaluation criteria of the
SEA, indicated in the document “Impact of emissions in saturated zones by
MP10 respirable particulate matter and MP2.5” fine respirable particulate matter (09/2023).
2 OBJECTIVES
• Generation of a three-dimensional meteorological field with the WRF model, to be used
as meteorological input for the CALPUFF pollutant dispersion model.
• Uncertainty analysis of meteorological modeling.
• Dispersion modeling of estimated emissions for the year of highest estimated emissions
of the Project with the CALPUFF model.
• Evaluation of regulatory compliance of the projected air quality, considering the contributions
of the different phases of the Project.

3 GENERAL METHODOLOGY
The modeling scenario presented corresponds to the operation phase of the Project (year 6 of
operation - higher emissions), considering as input the description of the Project in said phase. The
The results of the emissions inventory were inputs for the dispersion modeling carried out with the
CALPUFF model. The meteorology used as input to CALPUFF corresponds to that generated with the
WRF mesoscale model for the year 2022 and described in section 4. With the modeling of the
scenario, the contributions of these to air quality will be determined, which are subsequently added
to the measured air quality, to project the air quality when the project is in
operation.
This report also describes the configuration and meteorological simulation carried out with the
WRF forecast model and an uncertainty analysis is carried out on said modeling, comparing the
results of meteorological parameters at surface level with respect to data measured at a station
meteorological monitoring.

4 METEOROLOGICAL MODEL
4.1 Justification of the meteorological model
Following the guidelines of the Guide for the use of Air Quality Models in the SEIA, the selection
The meteorological model used in the present study was carried out based on the conditions
of the modeling domain of the area around the project, which corresponds to complex topography together
with the existence of a coastal edge, in this case the Guide recommends a model that allows simulating
a heterogeneous meteorology. For this reason, the hourly three-dimensional meteorological field of the year
The entire 2022 was generated using the WRF meteorological model, version 4.4.1.
Subsequently, the data were processed with MMIFv4 to be used in the quality model.
CALPUFF air. The digital meteorological files are presented in Appendix 4-1.1 of the EIA.
4.2 Description of the meteorological model
The Weather Research and Forecasting (WRF) model is a
Next generation mesoscale numerical prediction system, designed to serve forecasts
operational and for studying the atmosphere.
Four nested domains were created with grid resolutions of 27, 9, 3 and 1 kilometer
correspondingly, which are shown in Figure 4-1, in red the modeling domain is indicated
smaller, which is used to feed the CALPUFF dispersion model. Table 4-1 and Table
4-2 show the definition of the nested domains used in WRF and the geographic projection,
respectively.

5 AIR QUALITY MODEL
5.1 Air quality model justification
In accordance with the provisions of the Guide for the use of Air Quality Models in the SEIA, second
edition, the selection of the Air Quality model is based on the complex topography of the area where it is
location of the project and the scope of the emissions. For this reason, the model used was a type model
“Puff” as it is CALPUFF.
5.2 Air quality model description
The simulation of the project's contribution to pollutant concentrations will be carried out using the
CALPUFF model, recommended in the Guide for the use of Air Quality Models in the SEIA, second
edition.
CALPUFF is an advanced modeling system for air quality that also considers the
non-permanent meteorology. Its development was carried out by the “Sigma Research Corporation”,
while its current maintenance is the responsibility of Exponent17. Due to its performance,
CALPUFF was listed by the USEPA as a recommended model for evaluating the impact on
air quality of different types of projects, especially those where it is necessary to consider the
variation in time and space of meteorological conditions and their impact on transportation,
transformation and removal of contaminants.
The modeling system is composed of the following main components:
▪ CALMET: is a meteorological model that generates hourly three-dimensional wind fields, in
based on surface records and height profile. Additionally, the outputs of this model deliver
information in the modeling domain about mixing layer heights, characteristics
surface and dispersion properties.
▪ CALPUFF: corresponds to a Lagrangian-Gaussian model of transport and dispersion of puffs
or “puff” emitted by the sources considered by the project. In this way, from the
emissions and meteorology information provided, the program simulates the process of
dispersion and transformation of contaminants, in a validity range from meters to
hundreds of kilometers.
▪ CALPOST: is a program used for post-processing the results obtained in the
CALPUFF modeling, allowing the concentrations in the receptors to be calculated according to the
averages required by each standard. In addition, it is capable of managing the data of each

7.1.1 Total concentrations
Total concentrations are estimated considering the measured baseline, plus the contributions
estimated by other projects that have a favorable RCA, but that have not been executed,
plus the estimated contributions of the Project under evaluation. The following equation summarizes the methodology for
calculate projected air quality.
CAP = LdB + OP + AP
where
CAP: corresponds to the projected air quality.
LdB: corresponds to the current baseline.
OP: corresponds to the contribution of other projects that have favorable RCA, but that are not found
executed.
AP: corresponds to the contribution of the Project.

available in the National Environmental Control Information System (SNIFA), of the
Environment Superintendence. Table 7-7 summarizes the identified projects, their status
execution, and subsequently the availability of air quality modeling is indicated in its evaluation
