1. BACKGROUND
Environmental Services and Projects S.A. (SERPRAM S.A.)
is hired by the Secretariat
Regional Ministerial of the Environment, Antofagasta Region, to provide services in the
consulting: “Maintenance, calibration and operation of monitoring stations
installed in the city of Antofagasta, for monitoring the air quality of the
contaminants Respirable Particulate Matter (MP10), Lead (Pb) and Material
Sedimentable Particulate Matter (SPM)”.
The monitoring program consists of carrying out air quality measurements, according to what
next:
 Respirable Particulate Material, MP10
 Lead in Respirable Particulate Matter MP10
 Sedimentable Particulate Material, MPS
 Chemical Elements of Interest Present in the MPS.
The main activities of the service can be summarized as follows:
1. Develop Rendic and Oncological Air Quality monitoring stations,
a semi-continuous monitoring campaign, using high-quality gravimetric methodology
volume.
2. Sampling the two MP10 stations, with a frequency of 24 hours
every three days, according to the current methodology, check the flows, clean the heads
and equipment, change the silicone on the impact plate and check the controller.
volumetric flow every three months with calibrated orifices.
3. Provide the filters for the two MP10 stations, in order to perform the
sampling with a frequency of 24 hours, every three days.
4. Perform the gravimetric analysis of MP10, for the filters monitored during the
campaign, in such a way as to obtain the concentration of said pollutant.
5. Perform chemical analysis on the MP10 filters to obtain the Pb concentration
present in the air. Carry out the chemical analysis according to what is established in the
D.S. N°136/2001, from MINSEGPRES.

four points inside the city of Antofagasta. Include chemical characterization

Manganese (Mn), Molybdenum (Mo), Nickel (Ni), Selenium (Se), Vanadium (V), Zinc (Zn),

2. METHODOLOGY
To ensure the quality of the data obtained, a program was established that includes the
following aspects:
 Calibration procedures for equipment used in measurement
 Operation, control and maintenance program
 Procedures for data processing
 Procedure for handling particulate samples
 Site location procedure for MPS
Finally, it should be noted that the operation of air quality stations is carried out
under the guidelines of the D.S. No. 61/2008, of the Ministry of Health, while the monitoring

Air Pollution Control; and in ASTM Standard D 1739-98 (Latest modification
year 2010) “Standard Test Methods for Collection and Measurement of Dustfall” (Settleable

2.2 Lead in Respirable Particulate Matter MP10
2.2.1 The D.S. No. 136/2000, Ministry General Secretariat of the Presidency, establishes the
Primary quality standard for Lead in air.
I) Annual Standard:
The primary air quality standard for the contaminant lead will be 0.5 micrograms
per normal cubic meter (μg/m3N), as annual concentration.
The annual primary quality standard for Lead will be considered exceeded in the
air when:
a) The arithmetic average of the annual concentration values of two successive years
exceeds the standard level at any monitoring station with
EMPB population representativeness, that is, places with contamination levels
representative of the air quality to which people are exposed.
b) Likewise, the standard will be considered exceeded if the annual concentration
corresponding to the first annual period counted from the entry into force of the
present standard, is more than 100% higher than the level of the standard, in any
EMPB station.
