1 INTRODUCTION
This document, prepared by Gerard Ingeniería Acústica SpA., contains the acoustic impact study and
vibration of the Project “Mining Development Project for the Extraction of Clays for the Production of Concentrate
Rare Earths”, (hereinafter Project), which will be located in the Biobío Region.
The activities involved in the construction and operation phases of the Project could be capable of generating
an increase in the level of noise and vibrations in surrounding areas. In relation to the above, sectors were identified
inhabited, determining a total of fifteen (15) sensitive human receptors, nine (9) points of interest
fauna and two (2) vehicle flow evaluation receivers. For this, the realization of two (2) was contemplated
measurement campaigns, the first of which took place between November 28 and December 2, 2022,
while the second campaign took place on January 18 and 19, 2024.
In addition, projections were made of the sound pressure and vibration levels expected for
the different phases of the Project, through mathematical calculations and assisted by software.
The estimated noise levels in each of the Project receivers are compared with the maximums
permitted as established by the current national regulations corresponding to Supreme Decree No. 38/2011 of the
Ministry of the Environment (MMA).
For its part, the noise levels generated by the vehicular flow and the vibratory emissions generated by
heavy machinery, were evaluated according to
provided in the regulations of the U.S. Federal Transit Administration (FTA)
Report, “Transit Noise and Vibration Impact Assessment Manual”, which establishes level limits according to the
classification of each receiver.
For its part, for fauna, the criteria indicated in the “Evaluation criteria in the SEIA: Evaluation of
impacts due to noise on native fauna”.
The evaluation was carried out under the guidelines indicated in the “Guide for the prediction and evaluation of impacts by
noise and vibration in the SEIA, of the Environmental Evaluation Service (SEA).
2 OBJECTIVES

Evaluate the acoustic and vibration impact that the Project could generate, as indicated in the Guide for the
prediction and evaluation of impacts due to Noise and vibration in the 2019 SEIA.

2.2 Specific objectives
▪ Identify receptors sensitive to future noise and vibration emissions produced by the project
in the sensitive sectors closest to its works and activities.
▪ Carry out noise level and vibration level measurements in the selected receivers.
▪ Identify and acoustically characterize the emission of the main noise sources participating in the

to comply with applicable regulations or guidelines.

b) Vibration evaluation criteria
In the case of vibrations, this standard identifies two (2) types of impact due to vibrations, the first makes
reference to the criterion of inconvenience to people, while the second to the criterion of damage to structures. The criterion of
“annoyance” is related to the vibration levels transmitted by the ground whose influence and perception can
generate “annoyance” in the influenced humans. The evaluation indicators are based on the speed of
RMS vibration, which has shown a better correlation with respect to the sensitivity of vibration in the body
human. Vibration speed levels (L) are expressed in units of decibels [dB] referenced to
v
1 [µin/s] or with a V prefixed to dB [VdB]. In general, the threshold of human perceptibility is 65 [VdB]. The criterion
Annoyance is in turn subdivided into a “General” criterion and a “Detailed Analysis”. The general FTA criterion,
It considers the number of daily vibrational events and classifies them into frequent, occasional and infrequent events.
▪ “Frequent events” are defined as when more than 70 events occur per day.
▪ “Occasional events” are defined as when between 30 and 70 events occur per day.
▪ “Infrequent events” are defined as when less than 30 events occur per day.

5.2.2 Vehicular flow noise
For the modeling of vehicular flow (mobile sources), the calculation is carried out according to German regulations
RLS-90, which is divided into two parts. The first of them refers to the main descriptor of the emission line of
a road, called LME25, and which corresponds to the immission level produced by a road at a point
located 25 [m] from the central axis and 4 [m] above ground level.
The prediction of this descriptor (LME25) uses the variables of:
✓ Light vehicle flow.
✓ Heavy vehicle flow.
✓ Average speed of light vehicles.
✓ Average speed of heavy vehicles.
✓ Type of surface (asphalt or gravel)
✓ Multiple reflection (caused by possible tall buildings on both sides of the road).
The second part of the standard refers to the calculation of sound propagation from the emission line, using the
LME25 as input data.
The RLS 90 fragments the emission line as small differential segments, taking each of them as
a point source. The propagation is carried out considering the specific divergence of each segment, integrating the
entire road and calculating the attenuations for the 500 [Hz] band.
The physical configuration is obtained from geo-referenced plans with elevated contour lines and homes. This
calculation process was assisted by a computational model implemented through SoundPLAN® software, the
which allowed us to characterize the physical environment of the Project and carry out the estimate according to the aforementioned method.
Finally, the resulting map represents the emission of the road and attempts to interpret said emission based on the
vehicular flows. The sound emission line is made up of incoherent and equal point sources that
They radiate acoustic energy of cylindrical propagation.

Construction activities can generate varying degrees of vibration, depending on the machinery.
used and the construction methods used, also considering that the operation of the machinery
It generates vibrating waves that decrease in intensity with distance.
Buildings close to these activities can be affected by vibrations, the effects of which vary from
almost imperceptible levels, such as low-frequency noise with moderate perception, up to damage to structures
or in some part of these.
Generally, vibrations generated by construction activities do not cause damage to structures,
However, they can reach audible and sensitive ranges in sectors with buildings close to the work site.
An exception to the above may occur in fragile buildings, where great care must be taken to avoid
generate damage.

10 CONCLUSIONS
This report provides the levels obtained from baseline measurements of sound pressure level and vibrations.
in sensitive sectors close to the site of the project “Mining Development Project for the Extraction of
Clays for the Production of Rare Earth Concentrate”, which will be located in the communes of Penco and
Concepción, obtaining fifteen (15) sampling points of noise level and vibrations that characterize said
sensitive sectors, nine (9) representative fauna points and two (2) vehicular traffic evaluation points.
The first measurement campaign was carried out between days 28
e November and December 2, 2022, while
The second campaign took place on January 18 and 19, 2024.
The applied methodology allowed us to determine the magnitude of the acoustic emissions that the Project will generate and, based on
of this, evaluate the potential impact that it could generate on its environment.
This evaluation was carried out under the guidelines indicated in the “Guide for the prediction and evaluation of impacts
for noise and vibration in the SEIA, of the Environmental Evaluation Service (SEA).
For this purpose, receptors located in the closest sectors of the Project were identified, for which
determined the maximum allowed.
