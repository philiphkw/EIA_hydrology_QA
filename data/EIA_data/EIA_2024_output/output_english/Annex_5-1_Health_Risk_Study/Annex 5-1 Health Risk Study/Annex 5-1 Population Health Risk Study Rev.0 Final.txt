1 INTRODUCTION
The Mining Development Project of Clay Extraction for the Production of Earth Concentrate
rare, hereinafter, indist
In short, the Project corresponds to the set of parts, works and actions that
will allow the construction, operation and closure of a mining site and a waste processing plant
clays, material present in the area, which, due to natural effects, is highly weathered,
with the presence of rare earths in clay minerals. The Project will be developed mainly in the
commune of Penco, Province of Concepción, Biobío Region, without prejudice to the fact that a minor part of the
Works are located in the commune of Concepción, on the border with the Commune of Penco.
The processing plant will have a capacity to process up to 320 tons/hour of clay
wet and produce approximately 3,100 wet tons per year of Rare Earth concentrate,
equivalent to 1,700 annual dry tons of Rare Earth concentrate. Added to the above, in
In summary, the Project considers three (3) clay extraction zones (ZE Victoria Norte, ZE Luna, ZE Maite),
one (1) disposal area for ballast and washed clays (ZD Neptuno), one (1) soil storage area
plant (ZAV), a clay processing plant (the Plant) and a stockpile for temporary storage
of material prior to processing; Likewise, it contemplates a 15 kV electrical line and its connection to
an existing line and the construction and/or improvement of internal roads to access the different
areas (see Figure 1-1).
It is important to take into consideration that only mechanized mobile equipment will be operated, such as
such as hydraulic excavators, front loaders and dump trucks, since the material of interest is
found superficially. The above is explained by the origin of the type of soil present in the area of the
project, of the residual type, formed from natural weathering by physical and chemical agents
of the rock. As a result of the above, the extraction does not require the use and/or storage of explosives.
to carry out blasting, nor are crushing or grinding processes of the material considered.
during processing in the plant.
It is important to take into consideration that the mineralogical matrix does not contain radioactive elements and, therefore
Therefore, the development of the Project does not contemplate the use or extraction of radioactive materials, nor is
relates to the extraction of elements such as Thorium (Th) and Uranium (U), since it only considers the
extraction of the rare earth content existing in the sectors of interest, which do not contain
radioactive elements.

The methodology considered to evaluate the impact on health, due to particulate matter exposure

The beta coefficients used correspond to those validated by the World Health Organization (WHO).
and derive from the meta-analyses published between 2019 and 2020, which served as the basis for the review of its latest
publication “Air Quality Guidelines” of 2021. These coefficients in turn collect the results of studies
epidemiological whose analyzes are carried out using regression models.
For acute effects, the methodology with the greatest consensus today uses GAM models.
(Generalized Additive Models) in which the natural logarithm of the number of events is modeled as a variable
response, and the pollutant as exposure, plus an offset variable that represents the natural logarithm
of the population, and confounding variables, such as natural time splines that control for effects
temporalities such as seasonality, and natural splines of temperature and RH.
In the case of cohort studies used to estimate the effects of chronic exposure, we have used
Cox regression models. In both types of models the coefficients are not directly
interpretable, or in other words the “beta” do not represent a measure of epidemiological effect. It is the
exponential of the beta coefficients which is interpreted in the case of the GAM-Poisson models as a
incidence rate ratio and in the case of Cox models as a Hazard ratio (Hazards or Risks).
in different translations).
Both measures can be understood as a relative risk (RR). That is, the exponential of “beta”
corresponds to a RR for an increase in one unit of concentration of the atmospheric pollutant
analyzed. So if exponentiation is done with base “e”, the product of the beta coefficient times the differential of
the concentrations of a contaminant between two scenarios, we will obtain the relative risk (RR) due to the
change in contaminant concentrations between the second scenario and the baseline.
The RR reported by most agencies and publications corresponds to the exponentiation of the number
transcendent e, to the product of “beta” times 10 ug/m3. For example, the effect on overall mortality less
external causes, of an increase of 10 ug/m3 in exposure
chronic ion to the pollutant PM2.5/PM10, corresponds

increase of 10 ug/m3 of PM2.5 as an average chronic exposure produces a risk of 1.08 times and for

5.6 UNCERTAINTY ANALYSIS
Among the sources of uncertainty in this evaluation, it is possible to highlight:
a. Biases in CALPUFF modeling of concentrations from excess emissions
registered. It is generally accepted that the model tends to overestimate hourly concentrations
maximums in a way that could bias the effects towards estimates that are higher than the actual ones. Given
that, in this case, it is confirmed that there are no significant effects on health, this bias ensures and delivers
greater robustness to these conclusions.
b. The analysis indicates that the estimates most likely overestimate the effects when using
βeta coefficients that come from studies in European and North American cities that
present environmental contamination by particulate matter less than those observed in our
country. The HRAPIE document notes that the beta coefficients are higher in cities with
lower concentrations, reducing as the concentration of MP2.5 increases. This could
have implications in this study, since it is likely that the beta and the consequent relative risk
is lower, which confirms that a conservative analysis has been applied.
c. Finally, there is the uncertainty that could exist if the choice of the year for which the
base situation is not sufficiently representative of the baseline health situation and therefore the
Project contributions are minimized. On the other hand, it is likely that the years of the COVID pandemic
also generate a starting point that will not be representative of the effects that the Project
will generate in the future. For this reason, the health effects data from the period 2015-2019 have been used to
constitute the base scenario.
