1 INTRODUCTION
The impact evaluation of this Project (Chapter 4) recognizes the significant impact on the
flora and vegetation component, specifically impact due to “Loss of flora specimens”.
Additionally, the significant impact of fauna is recognized in “Loss of fauna specimens of
interest and/or sensitive” and “Loss and/or modification of habitat of species of interest and/or sensitive”
The project presents mitigation and compensation measures for the aforementioned impacts (Chapter 7).
Specifically, compensation measures are designed to produce an alternative positive effect and
equivalent to an adverse effect identified as a residual impact (that which was not possible to mitigate
or repair). A correct compensation measure is one that results in a gain in
biodiversity greater than the significant impact that will be produced by the execution of the Project.
This document identifies the residual impacts and estimates the gain in biodiversity.
of the proposed compensation measure, as established in the Methodological Guide for the
Biodiversity Compensation in Terrestrial and Continental Aquatic Ecosystems (SEA, 2023).
2 OBJECTIVES
• Identify the residual impacts on biodiversity generated by the Project.
• Calculate the loss in biodiversity of the residual impact considering the metric proposed in the
Methodological Guide for Biodiversity Compensation in Terrestrial and Aquatic Ecosystems
Continentals (SEA, 2023).
• Calculate the gain in biodiversity from the compensation measures considering the metric
proposed in the Methodological Guide for Biodiversity Compensation in Ecosystems
Terrestrial and Continental Aquatic (SEA, 2023).

4 METHODOLOGY: CRITERIA OF THE COMPENSATION GUIDE (SEA, 2023)
The Biodiversity Compensation Guide defines criteria to achieve a compensation standard
appropriate, specifically:
1.- Adherence to the hierarchy of measures: sequential application of measures to avoid, minimize, repair
and compensate for the affected elements of biodiversity. The above can be seen in the following graph.
Figure 4-1. Hierarchy of measurements and desirable relative magnitude in the application of measurements
Source: SEA, 2023
The purpose of mitigation measures is to avoid or reduce the adverse effects of the project.
or activity, whatever its execution phase, and include:
Repair measures are intended to replace one or more of the components or
elements of the environment to a quality similar to what they had before the impact
on said component or element or, if this is not possible, restore its properties
where impacts are expected to occur. Therefore, remedial measures involve
actions that improve the state of the environmental element impacted by the project, carrying out
within the area
of influence of such component
Compensation measures are intended to produce or generate a positive effect
alternative and equivalent to an identified adverse effect, which cannot be mitigated or repaired in
the area of influence; That is, they are measures that take care of residual impacts of the project
on biodiversity as a whole. In this way, the actions and effects of compensations
They materialize outside the area of influence of the impacted biodiversity.

2.- Equivalence requirement: the affected biodiversity elements must be compensated by
elements of similar characteristics, class, nature, quality and function.
3.- Additionality: it must be demonstrated that the compensation measure generates a gain of
biodiversity in contrast to a scenario without compensation, and that such gain would not occur
naturally in the absence of the compensation measure.
4.- Existence of limits for compensation: there are limits for what can be compensated, which
It is determined from the irreplaceability and vulnerability of the elements of biodiversity
affected.

6.3 Justification of compensation measures
From a flora and vegetation point of view, the justification of the set of measures refers to compensating
the surface of native forest formations to be intervened and segmented by the development of the
Project, considering both the high fragmentation that these vegetation formations present,
mainly due to the establishment of forest plantations, as well as the low percentage
of protection that they present at the national level. This compensation also includes the species in
conservation category whose affectation corresponds to a significant impact.
Additionally, it seeks to compensate the surface of native forest to be intervened by the Project,
considering the voluntary management and conservation of the environmental heritage corresponding to the
current and future BN and BNP formations, located within the area of influence of the Project, together
with those species in conservation category belonging to these formations (Gomortega keule,
Pitavia punctata and Citronella mucronata). These plant units, mainly the BNP, are found
highly fragmented by the historical intervention of the sector (forest plantations, industries,
fires, among others), in addition, due to the low percentage of protection they present at the local level and
regional, that is why its conservation is of vital importance to ensure its continuity.
Among the advantages of creating a conservation area is the protection of nature for the
future generations, remaining encumbered with the land, so future owners must comply with
the agreement; protects conservation values from threats due to changes in land use, formalizes the
relationship with the guarantor organization, which acts as a partner in conservation; the owner remains
owner of the property, among others.
Additionally, this measure is in line with the Queule Recovery, Conservation and Management Plan
(Gomortega keule), which has been promulgated by the Ministry of the Environment (MMA), through
Exempt Resolution No. 1,367 of December 9, 2020. Within the plan, the following are considered:
lines of action that are consistent with the proposed measure:
• Strengthening the evaluation and protection of the species in the SEIA
• Design and implementation of a restoration and protection program for the Queule
• Increase in public or private protected areas and incorporation into territorial regulations
• Promotion and development of research for monitoring the species and its habitats
Regarding the fauna measure, this is based on the fact that those forest plantations that have
Certain characteristics – such as the presence of undergrowth – are capable of becoming habitat for
species of native mammals such as Pudu puda, Leopardus guigna, Galictis cuja, among others (Simonetti
et al 20138). This proposal follows the idea of designing policies that promote environmentally friendly practices.
