1 INTRODUCTION
This document, prepared by Gerard Ingeniería Acústica SpA., contains the Baseline report of
noise and vibrations of the Project “Mining Development Project for the Extraction of Clays for the Production of
Rare Earth Concentrate”, (hereinafter Project), which will be located in the Antofagasta Region.
The activities involved in the construction and operation phases of the Project could be capable of generating
an increase in the level of noise and vibrations in surrounding areas. In relation to the above, sectors were identified
inhabited, determining a total of fifteen (15) sensitive human receptors, nine (9) points of interest
fauna and two (2) vehicle flow evaluation receivers. For this, the realization of two (2) was contemplated
measurement campaigns, the first of which took place between November 28 and December 2, 2022,
while the second campaign took place on January 18 and 19, 2024.
With the measurements carried out, a basal record of the levels of background noise and vibrations in the
different points distributed in the vicinity of the Project.
The measurements were carried out under the guidelines indicated in the “Guide for the prediction and evaluation of impacts by
noise and vibration in the SEIA”, of the Environmental Evaluation Service (SEA).
2 OBJECTIVES
2.1 General objective
Obtain background noise and vibration levels in nearby sectors that could be affected by the
construction and operation of the Project.
2.2 Specific objectives
▪ Identify receptors sensitive to future noise and vibration emissions produced by the Project
in the sensitive sectors closest to its works and activities.
▪ Carry out noise level and vibration level measurements in the selected receivers.
▪ Determine the maximums allowed according to national and reference regulations.

3.3 Evaluation of effects on fauna
Evaluation criterion in the SEIA: Evaluation of impacts due to noise on native fauna
This technical document “Evaluation criteria in the SEIA: Evaluation of impacts due to noise on native fauna”,
indicates that according to what is established in letter b) of article 11 of Law No. 19,300, a study of
environmental impact to evaluate the significance of adverse effects of noise impact on native fauna.
In accordance with what is stated in letter e) of article 6 of the SEIA Regulations to consider an adverse effect
significant particularly for impacts associated with noise, “the difference between the
noise levels with the project and the background noise level representative and characteristic of the environment where it is
concentrate native fauna associated with habitats of relevance for their nesting, reproduction or feeding.”
In this context, the object of protection will be understood as the habitat of relevant fauna for nesting,
reproduction or feeding, which must be found within the Area of Influence (AI) of the project, which is
must be defined considering the scenario with the greatest exposure to noise. Additionally, an area must be determined
representative for the object of protection (relevant habitat) on the AI as indicated in the Guide for the
Description of the Soil, Flora and Fauna Components of Terrestrial Ecosystems in the SEIA (SEA, 2015) due to
which is a dynamic component due to its movement in the environment.
To predict impacts, the prediction models indicated in the Prediction Guide must be used.
and Evaluation of Impacts by Noise and Vibration in the SEIA, (SEA, 2019), as appropriate to the noise sources
that contemplates the project or activity for each of its phases.
To evaluate the impact, the estimated noise levels must be compared with impact thresholds for each
species, which are determined according to the species evaluated, the noise source (continuous, impulsive or intermittent)
and the associated adverse effect (physiological and behavioral). Furthermore, they must be determined and represented
cartographically “affected areas”, which correspond to geographical areas around the project where
exceed the thresholds associated with physiological effects and behavioral effects, defined for each species.
which must be contrasted with the area where the receivers are located.
Once it is identified that there is a difference between the noise levels with the project and the noise levels of
characteristic and representative background of the relevant habitat, it must be kept in mind that:
▪ When the receivers have a level of noise exposure higher than the impact thresholds for
physiological effects, either as a species or taxonomic group according to the information available, are
will consider that the impact caused is significant.
▪ When the receivers present a noise exposure level that exceeds the reference thresholds
associated with behavioral effects, the significance of the impact on the species must be evaluated
evaluated based on what is stated in article 6 of the SEIA Regulations:

4 PROJECT DESCRIPTION AND MEASUREMENT POINTS
4.1 Project Description
The Project corresponds to the set of works and activities that will allow the construction and operation of a site
mining and a clay processing plant, a material present in the area, which due to natural effects
It is highly weathered, with the presence of lanthanides adsorbed on clay minerals.
The plant has a capacity to process up to 320 tons/hour of material and produce approximately
3,100 tons per year of Rare Earth concentrate.

7 CONCLUSIONS
This report provides the levels obtained from baseline measurements of sound pressure level and vibrations.
in sensitive sectors close to the site of the project “Clay Extraction Project for Production
of Rare Earth Concentrate”, which will be located in the communes of Penco and Concepción, obtaining fifteen
(15) noise and vibration level sampling points that characterize said sensitive sectors, nine (9) points
representatives of fauna and two (2) vehicular traffic evaluation points. The first measurement campaign was
It was carried out between November 28 and December 2, 2022, while the second campaign was developed
on January 18 and 19, 2024.
The applied methodology allowed us to determine the magnitude
of the acoustic emissions that the Project will generate and, from
of this, evaluate the potential impact that it could generate on its environment.
This evaluation was carried out under the guidelines indicated in the “Guide for the prediction and evaluation of impacts
for noise and vibration in the SEIA, of the Environmental Evaluation Service (SEA).
For this purpose, receptors located in the closest sectors of the Project were identified, for which
determined the maximum allowed.
During the daytime period, the recorded NPS vary between 35 and 63 [dB(A)], where the main noise sources
