1 INTRODUCTION
This report reports on the results of the environmental characterization of the heritage component
archaeological culture developed within the framework of the Environmental Impact Study of the “Development Project
Clay Extraction Miner for the Production of Rare Earth Concentrate” (hereinafter, the
“Project”), whose Owner is REE Uno SpA (hereinafter the “Owner”), in accordance with the provisions of the article
18, letter e.5) of the D.S. N°40/2012 of the Ministry of the Environment, which establishes the System Regulations
of Environmental Impact Assessment.
The Project, located in the Communes of Penco and Concepción, Province of Concepción, Biobío Region (see
Figure 1), consists of the construction and operation of a mining site and a Commercial Clay Plant,
material present in the area, which due to natural effects is highly weathered, with presence
of lanthanides adsorbed on clay minerals.
Lanthanides are 15 elements: lanthanum (La), cerium (Ce), praseodymium (Pr), neodymium (Nd), promethium (Pm),
samarium (Sm), europium (Eu), gadolinium (Gd), terbium (Tb), dysprosium (Dy), holmium (Ho), erbium (Er), thulium (Tm), ytterbium
(Yb) and lutetium (Lu). The elements named above along with yttrium (Y) and scandium (Sc) are
known as Rare Earths.
Of the 17 elements mentioned above, 15 of them are present in this project.
in the mineral, which are: lanthanum (La), cerium (Ce), praseodymium (Pr), neodymium (Nd), samarium (Sm), europium
(Eu), gadolinium (Gd), terbium (Tb), dysprosium (Dy), holmium (Ho), erbium (Er), thulium (Tm), ytterbium (Yb), lutetium (Lu) e
yttrium (Y).
The planned Commercial Plant has a capacity to process up to 320 tons/hour of clays and
produce approximately 3,100 tons per year of Rare Earth concentrate.
In addition to the above, the Project considers three (3) clay extraction zones (ZE Victoria Norte, ZE Luna,
ZE Maite), one (1) disposal area for waste and washed clays (ZD Neptuno), one (1) storage area
of topsoil (ZAV), an electrical line and its connection to an existing line and the construction of internal roads
to access the different areas. In addition, the implementation of temporary works is considered,
corresponding to work facilities, for the construction and authorization of the Project works, the
that will be located entirely within the intervention area of the Processing Plant, so
The total area of the Project is 169.19 ha, the details of which are presented later in section 3 of the
present document.
It is important to take into consideration that the ZE will be operated only with mobile equipment, such as
excavators, front loaders and trucks. The above, given that the material of interest is
superficially (in the outermost part of the Earth's crust) and corresponds to a weathered unit.
Thanks to this, the extraction does not require the use or storage of explosives to carry out blasting or
nor are material comminution processes (crushing and/or grinding) considered during the
processing in the plant.
The present study of the Archaeological Cultural Heritage component incorporated both the search for
documentary background such as the recognition of the Project area on the ground, in order to establish
the presence or absence of resources of heritage value within the Project works.

2 OBJECTIVES
2.1 GENERAL OBJECTIVE
The objective of this study is to characterize the cultural heritage component
archaeological located within the area of influence of the Project based on a superficial visual inspection
and a background check, in order to identify the presence or absence of elements
archaeological sites inside and propose measures for eventual archaeological finds based on
its location with respect to the Project works.
2.2 SPECIFIC OBJECTIVES
• Review the archaeological bibliographical background available for the Project area, with
in order to determine the presence or absence of previously reported assets
and its location with respect to the Project area.
• Carry out a visual pedestrian inspection in the Project Archeology Area of Influence,
focused on the in situ detection of finds of heritage value, as well as the detection and
evaluation of the status of records previously reported in bibliography.
• Determine the characteristics of possible archaeological finds or sites.
• Evaluate the impact that the Project works will have on the eventual records
archaeological.
• Propose measures for the adequate recording and/or treatment of possible findings, in
function of the current regulations regarding heritage.
3 DEFINITION OF THE AREA OF INFLUENCE
To define the Area of Influence of the Cultural Heritage component, the “Evaluation Guide” was used.
of Environmental Impact – National Monuments belonging to the Cultural Heritage in the SEIA” (SEA,
2012) and the “Guide on the Area of Influence in the Environmental Impact Assessment System” (SEA, 2017),
both prepared by the Environmental Evaluation Service (SEA), which allow defining the area of
influence as indicated by the D.S. No. 40/2012 (which establishes the Regulations of the Evaluation System
Environmental of the Ministry of the Environment, in force since December 24, 2013), which indicates what
following in letter a) of Article 2: “…The geographical area or space, whose attributes, natural elements or

as Area of Influence “… to the area where the project is located, including its permanent works, semi

archaeological”.

4 DEFINITIONS
In defining the findings, the postulates of Borrero and collaborators were taken into consideration.
(1992), for whom:
“…an “archaeological site” corresponds to a group of at least 10 elements associated
among themselves according to specialty, ergology and context; a “material concentration” refers to 2 to 10
units; and an “isolated find” corresponds to an archaeological object on the surface, without association
to any cultural element.”
Added to this classification are elements described in the Archaeological Procedure Guide (2020), such as
like:
• Immovable structure or feature, related to an intentional accumulation of stones or other
materials with various purposes that can form enclosures recognized as human construction
to inhabit permanently or temporarily or set of enclosures that involves the group of
human constructions such as village type.
• Funerary site, referring to the set of funerary contexts or structures and/or that contain remains.
human bones.
• Linear feature, related to any visible human traffic course on the terrain, such as
roads, cattle tracks, cart tracks, trails, railway lines, telegraph lines, among
others.
5 LEGAL FRAMEWORK
The present study is framed in the provisions of the current Chilean environmental regulations, which establishes a
series of regulations relating to the protection and study of cultural heritage, applicable to any work that
entails the intervention of the soil and/or subsoil of the national territory and could potentially cause
the complete or partial impact of the natural and/or artificial elements that make up the heritage
culture present within the Area of Influence of the
Project (AI).
Below, the legal bodies involved in the protection of heritage are briefly summarized.
cultural.
5.1 Law No. 19,300 “On General Bases of the Environment”
This law is based on the principle of “The right to live in an environment free of pollution, the
environmental protection, nature preservation and heritage conservation
environmental[…]” (Article 1), considering the “Environment” as “the global system constituted by
natural and artificial elements of a physical, chemical or biological, sociocultural nature and their interactions,
in permanent modification by human or natural action and that governs or conditions the existence and development
of life in its multiple manifestations” (Article 2, Letter ll).

In geographical terms, the Project area, located in the Coastal mountain range of the Biobío region, is
It is located in the central-southern macro zone of the country, which extends between the Chacabuco range in the North and the

In climatic terms, the Biobío region marks the transition between the dry temperate climates of the area

The project location, close to the coast in the northwest sector of the region, has a climate

6.1.4. EARLY POTTER PERIOD (1,500-950 CAL AP)
This period is characterized by the appearance of the first great pottery tradition in the central-southern area of Chile,
called Pitrén Complex (Aldunate 1989, Adán and Mera 1997, 2011, Campbell and Quiroz 2015).
This cultural complex is characterized by having a hunter-gatherer way of life, with a pattern of
reduced mobility compared to previous periods, while its economy is based on
horticulture. The consolidation of the cultural tradition of ceramics is also recognized for this complex.
with phytomorphic and amphiobiomorphic styles, highlighting bichrome techniques of black on red (Adan and Mera
2011). The social organization of these human groups seems to be heterarchical, characterized by a high
variability and diversity of roles, status, showing high heterogeneity, but low inequality (Adam
and Mera 2011). The first large cemeteries in the region date from this period.
Several researchers postulate that the Pitrén groups would have been related to other pottery cultures.
northernmost of Chile, such as the Molle (Norte Chico), Bato (Central zone), Llolleo (Central zone) groups.
and from the trans-Andean slope, such as Candelaria (Northwest Argentina) (Dillehay 1990 and Aldunate 1989). The
Pitrén's similarities with Llolleo arise from the comparison of complete vessels, mainly jugs
symmetrical and asymmetrical, where similarities are observed at the level of unconscious practices or the “not
visible”, although they would also be observed in associations of shared decorative techniques and motifs. Without
However, Pitrén presents more variability, unlike Llolleo, which has more productions.
standardized. For Pitrén, 18 formal ceramic types are known, which include jugs, pots, glasses, bowls,
asymmetrical and complexly modeled jugs. A special type of ceramics from this period are the jugs.
called pichimetawe (miniatures), identified at the Licanco Chico and Lof Mahuida sites in Temuco
(Adam et al. 2016).
Pitrén ceramics are characterized by being monochrome, with thin walls, with the use of negative paint,
of globular shapes with zoomorphic, phytomorphic and anthropomorphic designs, highlighting the decoration
through eyes shaped like coffee beans. Human burials during this phase present a position
lateral bending, accompanied by ceramic. The careful study of evidence from cemetery sites
has confirmed the assumption that they would be settlements separate and distant from the habitation sites,
although a direct and spatial relationship has not yet been determined (Adan et al. 2016).

reinforced by lithic evidence of the period, which accounts for the use of volcanic and siliceous rocks

vast spheres of interaction (Adan et al. 2016).

As a result of cultural fusion, the historical Mapuches emerged (Aldunate 1989). In the case of Isla

12th century to the 17th century, without this meaning the absence of significant changes.

This caused the coexistence between Mapuche and Spaniards to develop under the dynamics
Spanish, a situation that was consolidated after the Mapuche victory in Curalaba, in 1598, as a result of the

means a turning point, since space
border becomes a place of interaction,
generating cultural, economic, social exchanges and intense mixing, altering the configuration
existing until that moment. All this led to the omission or invisibility of the indigenous elements that
Before, they marked a greater presence in this territory (Fuentealba and Vera 2020).
The initial strategy of the Spanish crown was to assimilate the Mapuche population through work. many
of the conquistadors had been rural owners of modest economic situation in Spain and traveled
to America imbued with the mission of conquering territories for the Crown and converting the Indians to the Catholic faith
(Larrañaga 2016). They also sought fame and fortune for themselves; fame was achieved in military exploits and was
recognized through titles and honors, while fortune was found in the exploitation of gold and
the silver The indigenous population was incorporated into the work through the “encomienda”, in which the
Crown, represented by the Governors, placed groups of Indians in charge of the older Spaniards.
military or social prestige, who were responsible for its evangelization and care. In return, the Crown
He gave them the tribute that the Indians had to pay to the King as subjects (Larrañaga 2016).
In Chile the regulations of the encomienda were not followed rigorously. This was a territory without greater
importance for the Crown, due to its geographical remoteness and because riches were never found in
abundance. This laxity in the rules was an implicit compensation for the conquerors, for the rigors
what it meant to reside in this territory of poverty and war. This meant that in Chile the Indians worked
in the gold panners and on the properties of their encomenderos for almost a century after decreeing
the prohibition of this type of work. The most famous gold panner was Quilacoya, in the vicinity of
Concepción, which had between 10,000 and 20,000 Mapuches working for the conquistadors
(Larrañaga 2016).
In Chile, as in other borders where Spanish rule had not been able to break the resistance of
the indigenous people, the Spanish Crown authorized indigenous slavery during the years 1602 to 1627, however,
According to data collected by the historian Álvaro Jara (in Larrañaga 2016), the slavery of
Mapuches would have been carried out even before the authorization of the Crown. The Mapuches
Captured in battles were considered war booty and were assigned to work in the form of
encomienda, although there are also chroniclers who report on the sale of indigenous people.
In 1627 slavery was prohibited, the Hispanic-Creoles carried out a series of strategies to “obey”
such a prohibition, but that they did not mean in any way the freedom of the indigenous people, but rather the passage to another
forced labor regime. The living conditions of the former slaves did not change radically, since
Other forms of forced labor and deprivation of liberty persisted (Larrañaga 2016).
As a result of the above, a displacement of Mapuches from their reductions to the
ranches and the city of Concepción. Although from the beginning the migrations were not voluntary, as
It can be seen, for example, in the groups of Rere, Quinel, Hualqui and Quilacoya, since the last decade
In the 17th century, the influx of those who went to work in the ranches began to steadily increase.
Spaniards, and in some cases they moved so as not to return to their lands (Fuentealba and Vera 2020).

Later, on March 4, 1819, he decreed that the entire indigenous population was transformed into

Of all these processes indicated, there is an omission and an invisibility of the Mapuche element.

The Mapuches were condemned to live in reduced territories, where they could only practice a
small agriculture and livestock. And with the passage of time these spaces were insufficient for a
growing and impoverishing population, which is why a part of them sought better opportunities
inserting themselves into Chilean society as day laborers on agricultural estates or as labor in the centers
industrial (Fuentealba and Vera 2020).

7.5 RESULTS OF THE ARCHAEOLOGICAL VISUAL INSPECTION
In geomorphological terms, the Area of influence of the Project is located entirely on the flank
western part of the Coastal Mountain Range of the Northwest area of the Biobío Region, which has altitudes not
greater than 300 m.a.s.l. and slopes that fluctuate from low (0-2°) to very steep (over 35°) and
moderately steep (20-30°) (Chapter 3.5. Baseline Geomorphology of the Project).
Various inactive streams are observed in the Project areas, as well as a river course that crosses the
East to West Project (Estero Penco), which gives rise to the growth of dense plant formations in
around its channel. Likewise, the presence of properties with pine and eucalyptus forest plantations is observed.
in a large part of the Project area, in various stages of growth (from saplings to plantations
mature), associated with which the presence of wild forest of exotic species (Pine and
Eucalyptus) and the growth of shrub species and formations of the type of cover scrub that varies
from open to dense.
In general terms, the presence of forest plantations and the growth of bush vegetation,
trees, quila and/or blackberry associated with them, along with the presence of steep slopes and soils
eroded as a result of forestry activity, constituted access limitations that conditioned
archaeological inspection coverage strongly.
From a first reconnaissance of the land carried out between August 3 and 4, 2023, as well as
From the analysis of satellite images of the Project area, it was possible to recognize sectors with greater and
less potential for the development of a systematic surface inspection, considering limitations such as
slope and vegetation cover, so inspection activities were concentrated
mainly around the sectors that had medium to open vegetation cover and/or sectors
with recently felled forest plantations, which even without being completely cleared (since
showed remains of felling and clearing of trunks), allowed a systematic tour through transects.
Likewise, sectors with steep slopes (over 25°)9 were discarded from the inspection for reasons of
security, because these slopes, added to the soils eroded by forestry activity, with
presence of gullies and risks of landslides, meant a real risk to the development of the
inspection.

Based on the review of the different documentary sources consulted (registry of archaeological sites in
prioritized basins of the MOP, specialized magazines, books and conference articles, as well as files of
environmental evaluation available in the SEIA, among others), it was determined that there are no antecedents
of archaeological records previously reported within the Project area. The records
The closest archaeological sites, previously reported within the framework of the SEIA, are located at distances between
191 and 206 m of the Project works, corresponding to the isolated discovery “El Cabrito 01” - fragment
ceramic registered within the framework of a survey carried out on the occasion of the “El Cabrito Mining Project”,
subsequently entered into evaluation in 2018 under the name “BioLanthanides Project” (which was
subsequently abandoned) and to the “Landfill 1” site, reported within the framework of the EIA of the Project “Continuity
Operational Concepción Waste Management Center” (RCA N 202308101534-2023).
In a previous version of the Project layout, it was recorded that the archaeological site Fill 1 was located
located at a distance of 38 m from a planned road. Reason why it was decided to carry out a
request for intervention for the execution of drilling wells in the area, along with the characterization
subsurface of the PMP-SA-01 site, located inside the Maite Extraction Zone, authorized works
through the ORD. CMN N°1571 of April 3, 2024.
Due to the discovery of an archaeological deposit in the Project area that faced the Landfill site
1, the Owner decided to modify the layout of his works and move the layout of the road 150 m to the West
projected in this area, to reduce possible impacts on the archaeological component, therefore
that the characterization of the site was not continued, since it is no longer part of the Area of Influence of the
Project (specifically 206 m).
