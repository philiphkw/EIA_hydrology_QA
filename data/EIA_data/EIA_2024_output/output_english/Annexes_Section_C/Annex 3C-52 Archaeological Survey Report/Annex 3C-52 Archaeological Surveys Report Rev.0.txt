2 GENERAL BACKGROUND OF THE PROJECT
The Project, located in the Communes of Penco and Concepción, Province of Concepción, Biobío Region (see
Figure 1), consists of the construction, operation and closure of a mining site for the extraction and
clay processing.
For this, a series of permanent works are considered, namely, one (1) Processing Plant, three (3)
clay extraction zones (ZE Victoria Norte, ZE Luna, ZE Maite), one (1) ballast disposal area
and washed clays (ZD Neptuno), one (1) vegetal soil collection area (ZAV), stockpile for collection
temporary material prior to processing, a power line and its connection to an existing line and the
construction of internal roads to access different areas. The Processing Plant will have a
capacity to process up to 320 tons/hour of material to produce Rare Earth concentrate.
In addition, the modification of an existing section of approximately 1.22 kilometers of the
Hualpén – San Fernando oil pipeline, property of ENAP, for the exploitation of the Maite ZE. The
Project, in turn, considers the implementation of temporary works, corresponding to installations of
tasks for the construction and authorization of the Project works.
In sum, a total intervention area of 169.19 ha is considered, equivalent to the declared Area of Influence.
for the archaeological component (see Figure 2), characterized in Annex 3C-51 of this EIA.

3 ARCHAEOLOGICAL BACKGROUND OF THE STUDY AREA
3.1. GEOGRAPHICAL AND ENVIRONMENTAL SCENARIO
In geographical terms, the Project area, located in the Coastal mountain range of the Biobío region, is
It is located in the central-southern macro zone of the country, which extends between the Chacabuco range in the North and the
Chacao-Seno de Reloncaví channel, to the south, with the Pacific Ocean as its western limit and to the east
the Andes Mountains and the border with Argentina, administratively comprising the regions of
Valparaíso, Metropolitana, Libertador Bernardo O'Higgins, Maule, Biobío, Los Ríos and Los Lagos (Maldonado
et al., 2016).
In climatic terms, the Biobío region marks the transition between the dry temperate climates of the area
central and the rainy temperate climates that develop immediately south of the Biobío River. The area of
The project location, close to the coast in the northwest sector of the region, has a climate
humid temperate, with constant humidity with rainfall that fluctuates between 1,200 mm and 2,000
mm annually from north to south of the region. The native vegetation in this area of the region is represented by
thorns, associated with boldo, peumo and quillay, while in the southern section of the region the forest occurs
predominantly sclerophyllous (BCN 2023).
The bibliographic review presented below will give an account of the regional archaeological scenario, with
special emphasis on the coastal area of the Biobío region.
3.2. PALEOINDIAN PERIOD OR INITIAL SETTLEMENT (17,000 – 11,500 AP3)
To date, no evidence of this cultural period has been recorded in the Biobío region, so the
chronocultural framework is constructed as a sequence defined for the central-south by calibrated dates of
sites such as Monte Verde (Dillehay and Collins 1988), Alero Marifilo-1 and Pucón VI (Campbell and Quiroz 2015),
located in the region of Los Lagos and La Araucanía, respectively.
At a general level, it is understood that the human populations that inhabited the south of Chile in these years had
with an expeditious and poorly formatted lithic tradition, associated with the hunting of extinct fauna. It is necessary
point out that these technological and associative patterns were interpreted based on sites located in
valleys, like Monte Verde. However, mountain range sites, which share similar dates, are found
associated with modern fauna, fitting with the Archaic I models proposed by Cornejo and collaborators
for central Chile. In general terms, these Archaic I groups would not have hunted megafauna, counting
with an expeditious lithic technology, without the presence of diagnostic materials (Cornejo et al. 1998).
The Monte Verde site, near the city of Puerto Montt, corresponds to an open-air camp
shores of the Chinchihuapi Estero, which was occupied by hunter-gatherer groups in association with fauna
extinct, among which species such as Mastodonte and Paleolama stand out (Núñez et al. 2016). in the area
called Monte Verde II (14,600 calibrated years BP), organic remains of a structure were recorded

3.3. ARCHAIC PERIOD (11,500 TO 1,550 BP)
This cultural period is the most extensive within regional prehistory and has been subdivided into three
moments: Early Archaic (11,500-8,000 cal BP), Middle Archaic (8,000-3,950 cal BP) and Late Archaic
(3,950-1,550 cal AP).
In general terms, the Archaic period begins at the end of the Pleistocene, a time characterized by a
change in climatic conditions, with increased temperatures, added to the extinction of megafauna
and the consequent adaptations of the populations to the environment, observing groups of hunter-gatherers
with high mobility.

Based on subsurface characterization activities carried out at the PMP-SA-01 sites and at the edge
of the area facing the Fill 1 site, a homogeneous stratigraphy could be distinguished inside each
site, although, at the same time, clear differences between these two areas, which are distant by about 600
m each other.
On the one hand, at the PMP-SA-01 site, two stratigraphic layers were distinguished (named A and B), whose
Distribution varies depending on the location of the wells (inside or outside the existing road). By
On the one hand, in the wells located to the south of the road (inside the forest plantation) a first
layer (Layer A), corresponding to a silt-clay matrix, with a low sand content, semi-compact,
dark brown in color (which varies to lighter shades as the matter content decreases)
organic), which presents a high density of grass, eucalyptus leaves, insects, roots and rootlets during the
first levels, which then varies to low in the following levels (roots and rootlets). This has a high
humidity percentage, which drops to a medium level from level 4 onwards, while it presents a low
at zero density of inclusions (small rounded stones). It extends from level 1 (0-10 cm) to
variable depths (level 4 to 6) and does not present cultural materials, only fragments of tile
modern tailoring.
On the other hand, layer B (called layer A in wells P1 and P2, located inside the road, due to
that were previously excavated), corresponds to a semi-compact to compact silt-clay matrix,
reddish brown in color, with a low sand content during the first levels. Presents a percentage
humidity that varies between high to medium, while the organic matter content varies from high to
the first level (grass, roots, rootlets and eucalyptus leaves, although only in wells P1 and P2, which have a
small formation of vegetal soil in the first cm) to medium in the following levels (only roots and
rootlets). Like layer A, it has a low density of inclusions (subangular stones and
small-sized rounded, among them decomposing granodiorite – friable), which decreases in
the deepest levels. It does not present cultural materials, only fragments of modern-made tiles.
during the first levels in the P2 pit.
Except for a clay marble (possibly kaolin) – recorded at the surface level of the P5 well of origin
secondary – displaced by the unevenness observed in the area of ​​location of the
wells located in
the south on the slope of the hill – where a moderate to steep slope is recognized, added to the intervention
forestry of the evaluated area – no cultural materials were recorded inside the boreholes

Specific chronological and functional determination must be determined from specialized analyses.
