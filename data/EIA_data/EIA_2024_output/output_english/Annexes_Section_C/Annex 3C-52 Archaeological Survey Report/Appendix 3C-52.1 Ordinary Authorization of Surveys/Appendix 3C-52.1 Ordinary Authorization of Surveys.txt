A previous version of the Environmental Impact Study (EIA) of the Penco Module Project
was evaluated during the first processing in the SEIA in 2023 (CMN Entry No. 3025
of 05/16/2023), on which occasion the CMN presented observations to the project (Ord. CMN
No. 2684 of 06/28/2023). These included objections to the archaeological report submitted,
regarding its background study and the evidence of visual inspections in
terrain it presented. It is worth mentioning that the FSA sent annexes a new report
of archaeological visual inspection that corrects the observations of the Ord. CMN No. 2684-
2023, although this report will be evaluated within the framework of the SEIA, once it materializes
the re-entry of the project.
On this occasion, the information submitted by you informs that within the framework of the
preparation of the project's baseline, prior to its re-entry into the SEIA, was carried out
a new visual inspection of part of its area of influence (AI), where it was identified
1 archaeological site called PMP-SA-01. Your present request for characterization
subsurface includes both this site and the area adjacent to the Landfill-1 site, which
was identified and characterized within the framework of the EIA of the Continuity project
Operational Concepción Waste Management Center. This site is located just
19 m of the AI of the Penco Module Project, so your request considers the
preventive characterization of the Penco Module Project area that is immediately
adjacent to the Relleno-1 site; In both cases, the PMP-SA-01 and Relleno-1 sites
correspond to dispersions of fragments of ceramic material. In the case of PMP-
SA-01, a historical temporality of indigenous tradition is estimated.
Your request to archaeologically characterize the area adjacent to the Relleno-1 site,
responds to the CMN's request made through the Ord. No. 2684-2023, which
points out that “in relation to the archaeological sites registered outside the project AI,
at distances equal to or less than 50 m from any of the works, it would have been
requested to make a grid of boreholes of 1 m x 0.5 m at the edge of the area of the
project, in the area facing the recorded findings, distancing 10 m between
yes at most and considering a minimum of 5 boreholes (where at least 1
of them must correspond to a stratigraphic control well). The purpose of this
request is to verify that said archaeological sites do not extend towards the works of the
project, given the poor surface visibility conditions.”

II. According to the methodology and work plan, you propose to carry out the activities
archaeological in the following way:
1. At both sites, the excavation of 4 test pits of 1 m x 0.5 m is proposed,
and 1 stratigraphic control well of 1 x 1 m, totaling 5 drilling wells in
each archaeological site, separated by a distance of no more than 10 m.
2. In the case of PMP-SA-01, which you report is on a road that
presents a dense vegetation cover on both sides, the wells will be
distributed on the edges and center of said road, covering the total extension
surface of the site.
3. In the case of Fill-1, the wells will be arranged in the Project area
Penco module that is adjacent to the polygon already known for that site.
III. The CMN agrees with the objectives, methodology and work plan
proposed, which must be carried out in the manner established in the
request. Without prejudice to the above, it is requested that the activities consider the

1. The archaeologist holding this permit must be present during the performance.
of all archaeological work on the ground, being personally responsible
compliance with the proposed and approved methodology, as well as the
corresponding delivery of reports.
2. Regarding the composition of the team, the role of unit leader must be
exercised by graduates and/or graduates in archaeology, with the support of
personnel with training in archeology and/or support of workers without
training in the matter, who must be permanently supervised.
If necessary, professionals specializing in anthropology must be available.
physical, conservation or others, in order to ensure the optimal management of the assets
archaeological and its context.
3. At least 1 stratigraphic control well should be considered for every 5
survey units, which must reach the geological stratum culturally
sterile, regardless of the number of consecutive sterile levels that are
needed to reach said stratum. The units must be distributed in
different sectors of the site, following geomorphological criteria, variations in the
depth of the present deposit and in the depositional frequency, so
to characterize its stratigraphy clearly.
4. All units must reach the effectively sterile layer of the site to
be closed, according to control wells and cultural evidence documented in the
set of excavated units. This, in order to clearly delimit the
extension of the deposit in terms of its stratigraphic deposition,
thus ruling out the existence of funereal features or older occupations.
The wells can be expanded if necessary—maintaining the
defined methodology—in order to continue with its deepening.
5. If archaeological materials and/or features are detected during the
characterization, whether pre-Hispanic or historical, new ones must be excavated
units at a distance of no more than 10 m from each other, around the units that
present positive results, with the aim of adequately characterizing the
archaeological site. Likewise, if the number of established test wells is
insufficient
e for the effective delimitation of the site, the grid must extend
following the defined methodology, until having a minimum of 2 wells
consecutive sterile waste in each direction, always within the project area.

6. If there are areas with fills, the drilling wells located in said sectors
They must reach the original surface level of the land, to be excavated

7. The activities described in points 3, 4, 5 and 6 may be carried out within the framework

executive to present.
8. All wells must be excavated with archaeological methodology, so
manual, following the natural stratigraphy and controlling with artificial levels of
10 cm, along with screening 100% of the extracted sediment, which
It must be screened with a 0.4 cm mesh as a maximum measurement of
spacing.
9. If bioanthropological findings or other material features are detected
direct (e.g.: residential floors, structures, etc.) or indirect (such as
footprints, imprints, etc.), these must be registered and left on site for their
survey in later stages of rescue (after favorable RCA and with PAS No.
132), without prejudice to the fact that, if necessary, a controlled clearance may be carried out
of them for their spatial delimitation, but they may not be removed. In
In the meantime, they must be covered in all the walls and floor of the
unit with geotextile, shade mesh or other suitable material, to later
Cover with the same sieved sediments.
10. 100% of the cultural materials that are registered must be recovered
during archaeological work. Once these activities are completed,
must carry out the analysis of all the materials obtained, for
specialists in each materiality.
