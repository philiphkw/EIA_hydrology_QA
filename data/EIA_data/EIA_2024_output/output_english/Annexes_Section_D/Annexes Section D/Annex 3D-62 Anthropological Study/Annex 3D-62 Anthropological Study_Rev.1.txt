1. Introduction
Within the framework of the environmental processing of the Clay Extraction Mining Development Project
for Production of Rare Earth Concentrate, the characterization of
the organizations: Koñintu Lafken Mapu Indigenous Association – Penco and Cultural Group
Mapuche Lawen Mawida, both of functional urban character in the commune of Penco.
The Mining Development Project for the Extraction of Clays for the Production of Concentrate
Rare Earths, located in the Biobío region, commune of Penco and Concepción, is a
mining, along with a Material processing plant with a marked presence of clays, in
hereinafter called “clays to be processed”, which is characterized by having the presence of Rare Earths.
It should be noted that the plant has a capacity to process up to 320 tons/hour of
material and produce approximately 3,100 tons per year of Rare Earth concentrate.
The Project considers developing three (3) extraction zones: ZE Luna, ZE Victoria Norte, ZE Maite.
In addition, it considers one (1) disposal area for waste and processed clays ZD Neptuno;
It also includes a processing plant (the Plant), one (1) soil collection areas
plant (ZAV), electric transmission line and the construction and improvement of roads to
access the different areas. In addition, the implementation of temporary works is contemplated
corresponding to work facilities, for the construction and authorization of the works of the
Project.
On the other hand, starting in 2009 in the commune of Penco, the presence of
Mapuche organizations. One of the first to request and process legal personality in
CONADI is the Koñintu Lafken Mapu – Penco Indigenous Association and the other is the Cultural Group
Mapuche Lawen Mawida q
It was formed in 2006, under Law No. 19,418, which establishes standards
on Neighborhood Associations and other community organizations.
This report will characterize the life systems and customs of the GHPPI linked to both
associations, accounting for information collected through primary research techniques
social, also providing secondary information derived from other environmental studies in the
zone. The contents of this characterization are based on the provisions of Article 18 letter e.10

2. Objectives
General Objective:
The general objective of the anthropological report is to characterize in depth the systems of
life and customs of the Indigenous organizations located in the area of influence of the project,
in accordance with the provisions of the Regulations of the Environmental Impact Assessment System, in its
article 18 literal e.10 of Supreme Decree 40/2012 of the Ministry of the Environment and its
associated regulations.
Specific Objectives:
• Identify and characterize the social practices of land use, in addition to the
appropriation of natural resources.
• Characterize the modes of appropriation of the environment and the forms of production
associated with nutritional, medicinal, religious purposes, among others.
• Describe the forms of organization of Mapuche organizations.
• Identify and describe the belief system, cultural elements and cultural heritage
indigenous, tangible or intangible of both organizations.
• Identify and characterize the community's cultural practices, celebrations, places of worship.
interest and symbols of group membership.

3. Methodology and ethical criteria of social research
The study carried out corresponds to a qualitative and descriptive research. The
Qualitative approach refers to the use of primary sources of information from work
field, where we seek to know the “subjective meanings and understanding of the context
where the phenomenon occurs” (Vega et al, 2014, p. 524).
The descriptive method, for its part, is the procedure used in the social sciences to describe
the characteristics of the phenomenon, subject or population to be studied (Martínez, 2019). This type of
Research does not include the use of hypotheses or predictions, but rather the search for the
characteristics of the phenomenon studied that interest the researcher; In this case, the contents
indicated in Article 18 letter e.10 of the RSEIA. Descriptive research, unlike other
type of research, they carry out their study without altering or manipulating any of the variables of the
phenomenon, limiting itself only to the measurement and description of these (Cazau, 2006).
The preparation of this report considers primary and secondary sources of information. with

of the visits with leaders, members and lawentuchefe of the Koñintu Lafken Indigenous Association

4.1 Historical background, context, culture, territory
To confine the history of the first Mapuche to the south of Biobío, the perspective of
José Bengoa in his book, “History of the ancient Mapuches of the South” (2007), in which the writer
outlines and illustrates the events that gave life to the inhabitants of the time, their uses,
customs, ways of life and culture.
The Mapuche people are closely linked to the lake systems and river courses that descend
from the Andean foothills to flow into the immensity of the Pacific Ocean: the rivers, if
traditionally conceived as border milestones, come to be recognized as routes of
expeditious communication thanks to the development of efficient navigation systems, as was the case
of the canoe or wampo (boat made of a single log, which was used both for the sea and for the
rivers and lakes).
When reviewing various bibliographies on the history of Concepción, it is seen that the origin of the kingdom
of Chile identifies, to a large extent, with the long struggle sustained by the Mapuche people against
the Spanish occupation, around the so-called Biobío Border. The Hispanic presence, in fact,
After some initial vicissitudes, it was consolidated in Mapocho and the war was concentrated in the south. It
above, explains the historiographical prominence of this region in colonial times and, above all,
everything, in the early Conquest. The bravest Mapuche heroes and Spanish captains lived

4.2. Lands and territories for the Mapuche people
There are various readings and interpretations about the notion of lands and territories for the people.
Mapuche, which range from the legal, political, economic and cultural perspective. However, this
The report will highlight those that highlight the own or native views of the Mapuche people.
Among the readings that seek to understand the meaning that the ideas of territory and land acquire
As native categories, one can identify the Chilean anthropologists María Ester Grebe,
Sergio Pacheco and José Segura. In “Mapuche Worldview” Grebe, Pacheco and Segura (1972),
bring closer to the native categories of territories and lands. Thus, from the point of view of these authors,
The notion of “land has a sacred character,” and the Mapuche is located at its center. From this idea
It gives off an ethnocentric concept of the cosmos and the position of the Mapuche in it. They are the
men [sic] destined by “the gods to live in a certain territory” (1972:71). They identify
a “traditional coherent system” of land division, which is part of an ancient wisdom and

4.4. Koñintu Lafken Mapu Indigenous Association – Penco
Koñintu Lafken Mapu – Penco, corresponds to an urban indigenous Association whose address is
located in the commune of Penco, 12 km north of the city of Concepción, in the Region of
Biobío.
It is legally constituted and has its current legal personality, registered with N°184
in the Registry of Indigenous Communities and Associations of CONADI Biobío Region, in Cañete.
In its history, it was formally established on December 1, 2009 under the Law
Nº19,253 (Indigenous Law). The Association is made up of 57 women and 15 men, who are
partners. This means that 79.2% of the total partners are women. The composition of
The age range is between 78 and 31 years of age, with an average age range of 55 years.
