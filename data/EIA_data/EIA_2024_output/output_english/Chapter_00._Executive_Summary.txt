1 PROJECT DESCRIPTION
The Mining Development Project for Clay Extraction for the Production of Rare Earth Concentrate, in
hereinafter, interchangeably, the “Project”, corresponds to the set of parts, works and actions that will allow the
construction, operation and closure of a mining site and a clay processing plant, material present
in the area, which, due to natural effects, is highly weathered, with the presence of rare earths in the
clay minerals. The Project will be developed mainly in the commune of Penco, Province of Concepción,
Biobío Region, without prejudice to the fact that part of the works are located in the commune of Concepción, on the border with the
Penco Commune.
The processing plant will have a capacity to process up to 320 tons/hour of wet clay and
produce approximately 3,100 annual wet tons of Rare Earth concentrate, equivalent to 1,700
annual dry tons of Rare Earth concentrate. Added to the above, in summary, the Project considers three
clay extraction areas (called Victoria Norte, Luna, and Maite), a ballast disposal area and
washed clays (called Neptune), a vegetal soil collection area, a stockpile, a 15
kV and its connection to the existing line, and the construction of internal roads to access the different areas. Furthermore,
The modification of an existing section of approximately 1.22 km of an oil pipeline owned by
ENAP. The Project, in turn, includes the implementation of temporary works corresponding to installations of
tasks, for the construction and authorization of their works.

1.7 Description of the Operation Phase
The general process of the Project consists of the extraction of clays from the ZE by means of excavators and their
Transport by truck on internal roads to the processing plant. For its part, the ballast and washed clays,
They are taken to ZD. The operation of the extraction areas consists of the mechanical extraction of the clays by means of
the use of a hydraulic excavator, without the use of explosives or blasting, because these are in a material
weathered, and which corresponds to a unit that is distributed as a thick mantle between 40 to 60 meters
depth.
The clays that enter the plant will be processed to obtain rare earth concentrate by means of
use of extracting and precipitating agents, involving ion exchange processes, filtering, pH adjustment,
among others. The solutions obtained from the process will be treated in a water recovery system for their
subsequent recirculation, thus achieving a totally closed circuit without generation of Liquid Industrial Waste
(RILES).
The operation phase of the Project will last 6 years and considers continuous operation (plant, ZE, ZD)
for 365 days a year, 24 hours a day, estimating one day per month for preventive maintenance activities.
However, this will depend on the operational conditions, specifically, in the case that the processing

The raw material for the process is clay with rare earths, which is received at the Plant and fed to the stage.
of “Preparation and Classification” of the material for the separation of larger material (over 1 mm). Then,
is fed to a “Rare Earth Extraction” circuit from which a solution is obtained that goes to the production stage.
“Precipitation of Impurities” and a solution that goes to the “Solid/Liquid Separation and Washing” stage. In this
stage, a separation of solid and liquid is carried out, generating on the one hand washed clays that will be taken to the
Disposal Zone (ZD)
, a solution that goes to the “Rare Earth Extraction” stage and a washing solution
which leads to the “Water Recovery System”. The solution with rare earths and purified of impurities
continues towards the “Rare Earth Precipitation” area, where rare earths precipitate and separate to
produce a rare earth concentrate, which is packaged, stored and subsequently sent to
marketing.
The liquid phases obtained from solid/liquid separation processes are treated in a recovery system
of water to be recirculated to the process in order to generate a closed circuit to recover water and
reagents, optimize the extraction of the product from the material and reduce the necessary water supply in the
processing plant, thus leaving a reduced industrial or “make up” water consumption, in a closed circuit and
preventing the Project from releasing industrial liquid waste (RILes).

Determination: The area of influence is defined based on the sensitive receptors present in the environment of the

evaluation of impacts due to noise and vibration in the SEIA”, from the Environmental Evaluation Service (SEA), 2019”, indicates
that for the purposes of analyzing the effects of vibrations on human structures and receptors, the range
of interest is between 50 and 100 [VdB] (Hanson et al., 2006).
