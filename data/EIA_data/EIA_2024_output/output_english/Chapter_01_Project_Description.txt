1.1 Introduction
The Mining Development Project of Clay Extraction for the Production of Concentrate
Rare Earths, hereinafter, interchangeably, the Project, corresponds to the set of parts, works and
actions that will allow the construction, operation and closure of a mining site and a manufacturing plant
processing of clays, a material present in the area, which, due to natural effects, is found
highly weathered, with the presence of rare earths in clay minerals. The Project is
will develop mainly in the commune of Penco, Province of Concepción, Biobío Region, without
prejudice that a minor part of the works are located in the commune of Concepción, on the border
with the Commune of Penco.
The processing plant will have a capacity to process up to 320 tons/hour of clay
wet and produce approximately 3,100 wet tons per year of Earth concentrate
Rare Earths, equivalent to 1,700 annual dry tons of Rare Earth concentrate. Added to the
above, in summary, the Project considers three (3) clay extraction zones (ZE Victoria Norte,
ZE Luna, ZE Maite), one (1) disposal area for ballast and washed clays (ZD Neptuno), one (1)
topsoil collection area (ZAV), a clay processing plant (the Plant) and a
stockpile; Likewise, it contemplates a 15 kV electrical line and its connection to an existing line and the
construction and/or improvement of internal roads to access different areas.
It should be noted that, for this Project, the owner company, REE Uno SpA, has developed a process
pioneer and innovator of mineral extraction, called “Circular Mineral Harvest”, the
which presents several environmental advantages over other mining operations.
ras; that is, it does not use
explosives, does not require crushing or grinding processes and does not produce liquid waste or
massive waste generated by mineral processing. Furthermore, this patented process in
Chile, Brazil, the United States and China only use recycled water and recirculate about 95% of it.
For its part, the closure phase considers revegetating the areas intervened by the extraction zones.
and the disposal area, using native species, thus enriching the
ecosystems found in the Project area.
As indicated, the Owner, in line with the global and national challenge of decarbonization
of the planet, through traceable processes and together with a just transition, has innovated in a
unique Rare Earth production process, called “Circular Mineral Harvesting”
patented in December 2022. This process considers sourcing recycled water, providing

business and academia, including notable collaborations with the University of Toronto

The extraction and processing of rare Earths aims to meet the growing demand
associated with the optimization of equipment for electromobility, as its properties allow
more efficient and longer lasting engines. With the above, the country will be able to put itself at the forefront
of an industry of high global relevance, standing out not only for the value of the resource to be processed
(high quality), but also for the pioneering technology that will be used to obtain it.

rare Later in the closure phase, the Project considers the restoration of the surfaces

1.9.1 Project Overview
The Project consists of the construction and operation of a mining site along with a
processing with a capacity to process up to 320 tons/hour of wet clay and
produce around 3,100 annual wet tons of concentrate, equivalent to
approximately 1,700 dry tons per year of Rare Earth concentrate.
The Project considers 3 extraction zones, 1 disposal zone and 1 soil collection zone.
vegetable, a clay processing plant, a stockpile, internal roads, access from
existing routes, an electrical line with its connection to an existing line near Paso El Cabrito (km
71 Route 152) and the modification of an existing section of approximately 1.22 km of pipeline
existing Hualpén – San Fernando.
The process, which will take place in the processing plant, corresponds to a closed circuit of
continuous operation that begins with the reception and conditioning of the clays from
of the extraction areas. Subsequently, within the circuit, earth extraction is carried out
rare earths, precipitation of impurities, precipitation of rare earth concentrate and packaging of
product. The closed circuit, whose philosophy is based on the non-generation of industrial waste
liquids, is achieved by including a water recovery system.
The washed clays are transferred to the Neptuno ZD, specially enabled for these purposes, which
will consider the corresponding hydraulic works for the management and drainage of water, such as
such as, contour channels and surface channeling. On the other hand, for the connection of the
different facilities will be required internal roads that will contemplate, the construction of
sewers and/or speed bumps that will allow the passage of water at points where there is interference from
channels with said roads.
The Project contemplates the authorization of temporary works associated with the installation of tasks, the
which considers, among others, offices, waste areas, wastewater treatment system and
warehouses and facilities associated with the services that will be available to workers
during the construction phase.
The Project considers after the operation phase a closure phase based on the restoration of
the sectors intervened by the extraction and disposal areas, through revegetation with
native species.

1.11 Operation phase description (Art. 18 c.6.)
1.11.1 Overview
The general process of the Project consists of the extraction of clays from the extraction areas
by means of excavators and their transport in trucks along internal roads to the plant.
processing. For its part, the ballast and washed clays are taken to the disposal area.
During the operation phase, the non-intervention of the native forest will be protected, since,
None of the works and activities to be carried out are located in sectors with the presence of this type of
forest.
For their part, the native forest preservation units and isolated individuals in the category of
conservation (natural monuments), will be protected by a security strip of 30
m around these, which will be previously delimited in the construction phase.
The operation of the extraction areas consists of the mechanical extraction of the clays by means of
the use of a hydraulic excavator, without the use of explosives or blasting, because these are
found in a weathered material, and which corresponds to a unit that is distributed as
a thick mantle between 40 to 60 meters deep.
The clays that enter the plant will be processed to obtain soil concentrate
rare through the use of extracting and precipitating agents, involving processes of
Ion exchange, filtering, pH adjustment, among others. The solutions obtained from the process will be
treated in a water recovery system for subsequent recirculation, thus achieving a
totally closed circuit without generation of Liquid Industrial Waste (RILES).
The operation phase of the Project will last 6 years and considers continuous operation
(plant, ZE, ZD) for 365 days a year, 24 hours a day, estimating one day per month for
preventive maintenance activities. However, this will depend on the conditions
operational, specifically, in the event that the annual processing is less than expected, this
would lead to the operation of the Extraction Zones being extended over time, taking into account
consideration that it will not be possible to extract more clay than the capacity of the ZD
Neptune allows disposition.

By means of an anode cable, the steel core of the anode is joined to a direct current source,

ground, as well as the pipeline. These anodes are placed at a set distance from each other, so
according to the weight, material and useful life, which will be defined in the construction stage.
