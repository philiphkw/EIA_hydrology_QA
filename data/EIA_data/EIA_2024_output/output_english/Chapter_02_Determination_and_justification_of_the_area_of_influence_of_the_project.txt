2 DETERMINATION AND JUSTIFICATION OF THE AREA OF INFLUENCE OF THE
PROJECT
2.1 Introduction
Within the framework of the presentation of the Environmental Impact Study (EIA) of the “Development Project
Clay Extraction Miner for the Production of Rare Earth Concentrate” and in accordance with the
established in Law No. 19,300 on General Environmental Bases, and in Article No. 2,
letter a), of Supreme Decree No. 40/2012, modified by D.S. No. 30/2023, both from the Ministry
of the Environment (MMA), which approves the Regulation of the Impact Assessment System
Environmental (RSEIA), in addition to what is indicated in the Guide on the Area
a of Influence on the System
Environmental Impact Assessment (SEIA), referred to in Exempt Resolution No. 0423 of the
Executive Directorate of the Environmental Evaluation Service (SEA), dated April 26, 2017, this
chapter presents the background for the determination and justification of the area of influence of the
Project, both to rule out effects and to characterize the geographic area where
will generate significant and non-significant impacts on the elements of the environment that
They are subsequently described in Chapter 3 “Baseline”.
In this regard, the Regulation of the Environmental Impact Assessment System (SEIA) defines the
concept of area of influence in its Article 2, letter a) as:
“The geographic area or space, whose attributes, natural or sociocultural elements must
be considered with the purpose of defining whether the project or activity generates or presents
any of the effects, characteristics or circumstances of Article 11 of the Law, or to
justify the non-existence of said effects, characteristics or circumstances.”
In addition to the above, Article 18, letter d) of the same Regulation establishes that:
“The area of influence will be defined and justified for each affected element of the environment.”
environment, taking into consideration the environmental impacts potentially
significant about them, as well as the geographical space in which the parts are located,
works and/or actions of the project or activity.”
In addition to what is stated in the SEIA Regulations, the Guide “Area of Influence in the System
of Environmental Impact Assessment” of the Environmental Assessment Service (2017), points out, in its
Chapter 2, the elements of the environment whose areas of influence must be described and the
importance of these for the prediction and evaluation of environmental impacts. These elements of

2.2 Methodology
The determination of the area of influence for each component of the environment has been
structured based on schemes of conceptual models of “cause-effect” relationships, for the
identification of potential effects on the different environmental components due to the
parts of the works and/or actions of the Project, as stated in article 18, literal d), of the Regulations of the
SEIA. Likewise, what is stated in the “Guide on the Area of Influence in the
Environmental Impact Assessment System” of the Environmental Assessment Service (2017).
For the above, the following steps are considered:
• Identification of components of the environment that, given the activities and works of the
Project identified, could be altered by potential effects, taking as
Reference is made to article 11 of Law 19,300 on General Environmental Bases. The
identification of those elements of the environment that could be affected
results from a crossing between the activities or works of the Project that may give rise to
potential impacts (in each of its phases) and the elements of the environment
present at the Project location. For this, the chapter refers
to a brief description of the Project, including, among others, its location, emissions,
inputs, waste and, by the way, the parts, works and actions thereof.
• Determination of areas of influence for each environmental component possibly
impacted from the previous point, considering the potential environmental impacts
(from activities or works of the Project), the geographical space in which they will be located
the works or the activities will be developed, and using conceptual models of relationships
“cause-effect” and its singularities, if applicable. The justification of the area of influence
It is complemented with a brief description of it.
• When there are impacts regulated in such a way that a criterion or level of
impact considered admissible, the definition of the area of influence will consider the criterion
location of the receiver or the allowable limit.
• For those components where synergistic effects are identified, due to the existence
of existing projects, these will be considered in the scope of the area of influence.

It is associated with certain soil conditions (texture, depth), slope, aggressiveness

which are determined by the geographical space in which the parts, works and
actions contemplated in the construction and operation of the Project.
According to Iroumé (2016), cited by Unesco, 2017, vegetation reduces the speed and volume of the
surface runoff and favors infiltration, reducing the transport capacity of the
suspended sediments that are deposited before reaching the channels (Verstraeten et al., 2006;
Stutter et al., 2012). The results of their study show that vegetation strip widths of 3 and 6
meters were 81% and 90% efficient, respectively, in reducing sediment load
coming from the erosion of a slope 10 meters long and 20% slope, and that the largest
The amount of sediment retained occurred in the first 1.7 meters of vegetation. However,
Greater widths of the vegetated zone may be required to trap sediments from the
erosion of slopes more than 10 meters long. However, this may not necessarily be the case given
that Gomi et al. (2008), for example, highlight that, when comparing plots of different lengths, the
Surface runoff tends to decrease at greater lengths, since it occurs discontinuously
and with infiltrations along the slope, especially in well-drained land. According to the
background presented, we can assure that the defined criterion is conservative, since
protects the surface that is susceptible to starting the erosive process with the consequent
particle transport.

Project, where a large part of these slopes are greater than 15%; and the risks of activation of
