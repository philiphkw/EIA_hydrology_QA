3.1.1 Introduction
This section provides the characterization of the components of the Physical Environment, Climate and
Meteorology for the “Mining Development Project for the Extraction of Clay for the Production of
Rare Earth Concentrate” that enters the Environmental Impact Assessment System (SEIA) under the
form of Environmental Impact Study (EIA). The Baseline of Climate and Meteorology is presented in
in accordance with the provisions of article 18 literal e.1) of the SEIA Regulations, D.S. No. 40/2012,
modified through D.S No. 30/2023, both from the Ministry of the Environment, and as established in
Law No. 19,300 on General Environmental Bases.
3.1.2 Objectives

The characterization of climate and meteorology aims to make known the regional and local context
in which the rest of the components of the environment develop, as well as the conditions
in which the Project will be inserted.

- The characterization of the climate and meteorology of the project site area will allow
establish the type of climate prevailing in the area, and know the values and variations of the
main meteorological variables, such as: solar radiation, wind speed and direction,
temperature, relative humidity, atmospheric pressure and precipitation.

3.1.4.1 Climate
For the climatological characterization of the Project location area, a description was made
based on current bibliography, both at regional and local scale, the latter corresponding to the
associated microbasin. For the local climatological characterization of the Project location area
The Köppen classification (1948) was used, which proposes a combination of letters, with which it describes
different characteristics of the behavior of the precipitation and temperature variables throughout the
year. This classification divides the Earth into five major climatic zones, which are distributed from the
Ecuador towards the poles, and which are called with capital letters A, B, C, D and E.
Additionally, this classification system uses other capital letters to indicate some
climatic particularities. This is the case of what happens with dry climates (B) that are subdivided into climates
semiarid (BS) and arid (BW), or for cold climates (E) that include tundra climates (ET) and
those of ice (EF). In these cases, the letter is located after the main letter. In cases where the
influence of altitude is relevant, that is, above 3,000 meters above sea level, in the characterization of the climate, it is
makes use of the letter H, in first, second or third position, depending on the particularities of
each case (Köppen, 1948).
The next letter, in lower case, is defined by the rainfall particularities of the area in question.
These letters specifically refer to the dry season; thus the letter “f” (fehlt) represents the
absence of a dry season, the “w” (winter), existence of a dry season in winter, the “s” (summer),
dry season in summer and the “m” (monsoon), dry season determined by monsoon winds.

3.1.7 Synthesis and conclusions
The climate of the sector where the Project will be located, according to the Köppen classification, corresponds to
“Warm temperate with winter rains and high atmospheric humidity”, belonging to the type climates
temperate, which are characterized by having average temperatures of the coldest month below 18°C and
above -3 °C and seasonal rains.
Regarding meteorology, it was used
data from four monitoring stations near the site of
Project location: Kingston College, Liceo Polivalente, Coronel Norte and Nueva Libertad.
According to the stations described, the average wind speed does not exceed 3.66 m/s.
Calms, corresponding to winds with a speed of less than 0.5 m/s, occur mainly in
night time and present up to an average annual frequency of up to 18% at the Liceo station
Multipurpose. In relation to wind direction, most stations share components
predominantly coming from the West and the South during the day, while at night
The direction appears more heterogeneous.
The average temperature in the characterized stations is around 13°C, with
minimum (between -0.5°C and 0.1°C) and maximum (between 31°C and 32°C), with daily thermal amplitudes and
low seasonality, due to the proximity of the stations to the coast.
In relation to relative humidity, it is observed that it increases at night and decreases at night.
daytime as a result of solar radiation, the latter with maximums that occur around
noon.
Precipitation in the area is concentrated mainly in the winter months, with the exception of the
Liceo Polivalente station, where precipitation during 2020 reached its maximum in months
summer.
Regarding climate change projections in the study area, based on historical information
available and the projections of climate change models, a change in average values is expected
and extremes of the most relevant variables analyzed, which correspond to temperature and precipitation.
The days in which precipitation exceeds a high threshold (95% percentile) have been decreasing on a scale
annual, however, when analyzed based on climate change models, an increase is suggested
of precipitation events that exceed the record in these months. There is a potential
progressive increase in the days in which it does not rain, on an annual scale and the increase in extreme events
of precipitation in summer months.
The indicators linked to the increase in maximum temperatures have greater significance in summer and
on an annual scale, so increases in heat wave reports are expected based on history

This section provides the characterization of the air quality component, in accordance with the

The characterization of air quality aims to reveal the baseline quality of this air.
component prior to the development of the Project.
3.2.2.2 Specific Objective
- Establish a characterization of the air quality of the area of influence of the Project in relation to
with pollutants that have records in the sector, mainly particulate matter
(MP10 and MP2.5) and gases (SO₂, NO₂ and CO), with reference to primary quality standards and

3.2.3 Area of Influence
The area of influence is defined based on the sensitive receptors present in the surroundings of the works and
Project activities, and variations in basal air quality as a result of Project contributions
in its different phases. The existence of a population or human groups that could
be affected by an alteration in air quality due to the construction and operation of the Project.
The consideration of the distribution of the Project works and the recipients close to them responds to
the purpose of evaluating the possible effects of products of particulate matter emissions, and others
pollutants. The activities that could generate effects on air quality are those that
constitute sources of emission of particulate matter and polluting gases that tend to disperse
around the source, although covering limited extensions. The effects are directly associated with
risks to people's health, so nearby populated localities are considered to be
could be affected by the works and/or activities of the Project, in which the recipients are located
sensitive to the emissions generated by the Project in the construction phase. It is considered 1% of the
norms as a threshold, since below 1% with respect to the norms, these are contribution values
low enough, where the contribution alone should not generate effects or changes in condition
in recipients of interest.

3.3 Noise and Vibration Levels
3.3.1 Introduction
This section provides the characterization of the noise and vibration component of the area of
influence of the Mining Development Project for the Extraction of Clays for the Production of Concentrate
Rare Earths, in accordance with the provisions of article 18 literal e.1) of the SEIA Regulations, D.S.
N°40/2012, modified through D.S N° 30/2023, both from the Ministry of the Environment, and as
established in Law No. 19,300 on General Environmental Bases.
The activities involved in the construction, operation and closure phases of the Project have the
potential to generate an increase in the level of noise and vibrations in surrounding areas. In relation to what
Previously, inhabited sectors were identified, determining a total of fifteen human receptors,
nine points of fauna interest and two vehicle flow evaluation receptors. For this it was considered
the carrying out of two measurement campaigns, the first carried out between November 28 and 2
December 2022, while the second campaign took place on January 18 and 19, 2024.
In particular, the following
document characterizes the level of sound and vibration pressure of the area of
influence for this component, in the sectors where the human receptors and the
environments suitable for receptors of native fauna, which could be exposed by the eventual increase
of the level of noise and vibrations, under the guidelines indicated in the “Guide for the prediction and evaluation
of impacts due to noise and vibration in the SEIA”. With the measurements made, a basal record was obtained
of noise and vibration levels, to which the selected points are exposed, distributed in the
vicinity of the site where the Project works will be carried out.
3.3.2 Objectives
3.3.2.1 General Objective
Characterize and quantify noise and vibration emissions for the area of influence of the Project in its
base situation.
3.3.2.2 Specific Objective
• Identify receptors sensitive to future noise and vibration emissions produced by
the project in the sensitive sectors closest to the works and its activities.
• Carry out measurements of noise level and vibration level in the selected receivers.
• Determine the maximum levels allowed for each receptor, according to D.S. No. 38/2011 of the MMA.

3.3.8 Synthesis and conclusions
This report provides the levels obtained from the baseline sound pressure level measurements and
vibrations in sectors close to the site of the Mining Development Project for the Extraction of
Clays for the Production of Rare Earth Concentrate, which will be located in the communes of Penco and
Concepción, obtaining fifteen (15) noise level and vibration sampling points that
These sensitive sectors are characterized by nine (9) representative fauna points and two (2)
vehicular traffic evaluation. The first measurement campaign was carried out between the 28th of
November and December 2, 2022, while the second campaign took place on the 18th and 19th of
January 2024.
This characterization was carried out under the guidelines indicated in the “Guide for the prediction and evaluation
of impacts due to noise and vibration in the SEIA”, of the Environmental Evaluation Service (SEA).
For this purpose, receptors located in the closest sectors of the Project were identified, for which
The maximum permitted values were determined.

i. Coastal Batholith
It corresponds to a plutonic complex with lithologies between tonalite and granodiorite, with limited areas of
quartz diorite and granite (Gajardo, 1981). Named Southern Coastal Batholith by Hervé in 1987.
It crops out in a NNE-SSW strip to the east of the Accretionary Metamorphic Complex.
The intrusive units of the Coastal Batholith instruct the Eastern Series during the Upper Paleozoic
of the Metamorphic Complex, generating a regional contact metamorphism type Buchan (low pressure
and high temperature) that extends approximately 20 to 30 km parallel to the edge of the intrusive body
(Vásquez, 2001). Limited pegmatite outcrops are identified and characterized by different
authors (Collao, 2009; Velásquez, 2012; Jil, 2014) as late events in the crystallization process of
batholith. Locally, to the south of the Project area, it is possible to observe migmatization in the area of
contact of the intrusive with the Metamorphic Complex.
The rocks of the Costeo Batholith, in the areas near the Project, underlie the sedimentary formations
Quiriquina and Curanilahue.
ii. Metamorphic Complex
The Paleozoic Metamorphic Basement of the Coastal Mountain Range, which consists of a strip of about 800
km long and 30 km wide on average Aguirre (1972) divides the strip into two main units:
Oriental Series and Western Series, which differ from each other due to their dominant lithology, present structures and
metamorphic grade that affects them. The metamorphic units of the complex have been interpreted as
a paired metamorphic belt constituted by a series of low P/T to the east and a series of high P/T to the
west (Aguirre et al. 197; Hervé, 1988;). Currently, both series are interpreted as results of
a frontal and basal accretion, respectively, in an active continental margin context (Willner et al.
2005; Glodny et al., 2005, Glodny et al., 2008).
The Oriental Series is the dominant one in the study area, it is mainly composed of a
alternation of metapelites and metarenites of turbidite origin (Hervé et al., 2007, 2013), which were
deposited in a deep stable continental margin and accreted to the continent upon restarting the
subduction during the Ordovician (Glodny et al., 2005). The series is submitted, from
the west, to an accretion
frontal, which generates foliation in the metasediments and is responsible for a regional metamorphism of the
Barrovian type, expressed by pretectonic associations of staurolite, garnet and biotite (Vásquez,
2001).
Studies carried out by Hervé et al. (2013), the Oriental Series would have been deposited in the Lower Carboniferous,
before the Western Series. However, both would have been formed before the intrusion of the Batholith.
Coastal, to which they associate an age of 305-320 Ma.

of verticals in which depth and speed are measured. Partial areas and velocities are thus determined.

3.8 Hydrogeology
3.8.1 Introduction
This study is carried out within the framework of the project called Extraction Mining Development Project
of Clays for the Production of Rare Earth Concentrate (hereinafter the Project) of the company REE
UNO SpA, hereinafter ALARA (hereinafter the Owner), in accordance with the provisions of article 18 literal
e.1) of the SEIA Regulations, D.S. N°40/2012, modified through D.S N° 30/2023, both of
Ministry of the Environment, and the provisions of Law No. 19,300 on General Bases of the Environment
Environment.
The Project, administratively located in the Commune of Penco and Concepción, Province of Concepción,
Biobío Region, consists of the construction and operation of a mining site along with a
processing of weathered granitic material, with the presence of clays. The regolith, hereinafter
called “Clays to be processed”, it is formed as a result of high weathering of rocks and contains
lanthanides adsorbed on clay minerals. Lanthanides, or Rare Earths, are 15 elements whose
Atomic number increases from Lanthanum (57) to Lutetium (71). Due to their similar chemical behavior,
Yttrium (Y) is added to them. From now on they will be called “rare earths”.
The plant has a capacity to process up to 320 tons/hour of material and produce concentrate
of Rare Earths.
In addition to the above, the Project considers three (3) clay extraction zones (ZE Victoria Norte, ZE
Luna, ZE Maite), one (1) disposal zone for waste and processed clays (ZD Neptuno), one (1) zone
soil collection plant (ZAV), a clay processing plant (the Plant) and a stockpile for the
temporary stockpile of material prior to processing, an electrical line and its connection to an existing line and
the construction of internal roads to access the different areas. Furthermore, it is considered the
implementation of temporary works corresponding to work facilities, for the construction and
authorization of the Project works.
During its operation phase, the Project considers a sequential closure of the extraction areas. It is
That is, these areas will not come into operation simultaneously, so, once exploitation is completed
In each of these areas, revegetation begins.
For its part, upon completion of the disposal of the processed clays in the areas of di
position is also
will execute the closure of these through revegetation, using the vegetal soil available in the areas of
vegetable collection.

influence of the Project, having public and bibliographical background for this purpose.

3.8.6 Synthesis and conclusions
The location area of the Characterization Project is located in the Cordillera de La Costa, in which
Intrusive and metamorphic crystalline rocks with very low to no primary permeability emerge. In scale
Regionally, this area is defined as an area of zero hydrogeological importance (reference).
At the project level, it is worth mentioning that as a result of the climate of the area, temperate with periods of rain
well marked, in addition to the important vegetation cover, intense processes of
chemical and physical weathering in the batholith rocks, causing alteration of the mineralogy
primary to clays and the disintegration of the most resistant mineral grains.
The result of this results in a residual soil called Saprolite, which extends from
semi-horizontally, in a large part of the project area, and is where the resources of
Rare Earths. The saprolite develops extensively and predominantly, also conferring a level
with characteristics that strongly condition the infiltration and movement of groundwater.
The permeability in the Saprolite is conditioned by the important clay content, which motivates
that this horizon tends to act as an aquitard, even more restrictive since it does not present in
vertical presence of water; although in the most superficial parts the occurrence of
moistening of this soil, associated with climatic conditions, but without establishing a condition of
saturation and water flow. This situation is restricted to shallow and locally located sectors.
The unit made up of saprolite has very low permeability conditions that do not allow
a hydrogeological connection between the surface and the semifractured unit. In this sense, the saprolite
establishes a confinement condition that implies that the existing water is under pressure.
In the transition between the Saprolite and the basal rock (UH2), it corresponds to a unit composed of a
less developed saprolite, a mixture of a semi-chemically disintegrated and fractured rock, which gives it
infers distinctive hydraulic characteristics from the rest of the units, with greater relative permeability
with respect to the overlying unit. In fact, the occurrence of water is detected in this transition zone
deep between saprolite and healthy rock, where permeabilities are greater in various orders of
magnitude, however, of very low productivity.
The pumping tests carried out show that for extremely low flow rates (0.2 to 0.4 l/s)
It mostly produces exhaustion in very short times (between minutes and a few hours); what
indicates that it is a system of very poor water productivity, consistent with what has already been indicated.
