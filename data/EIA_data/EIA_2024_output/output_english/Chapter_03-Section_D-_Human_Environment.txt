3 BASELINE (SECTION D)
3.1 Human Environment
3.1.1 Introduction
This report describes the characterization or Human Environment Baseline (LBMH), developed in the
framework of the Environmental Impact Study of the “Clay Extraction Mining Development Project for
Production of Rare Earth Concentrate” (hereinafter and interchangeably “the Project”), consisting
in the construction and operation of a mining site along with a clay processing plant,
with a capacity to process up to 320 tons/hour of wet clay and produce
approximately 3,100 annual wet tons of Rare Earth Concentrate, equivalent to
1,700 annual dry tons of Rare Earth concentrate.
The Project, located in the communes of Concepción and Penco in the Biobío region, considers a
capacity to process up to 320 tons/hour of wet clay and produce approximately 3,100
annual wet tons of Rare Earth concentrate, equivalent to 1,700 annual dry tons
of Rare Earth concentrate. Added to the above, in summary, the Project considers three (3) zones of
clay extraction (ZE Victoria Norte, ZE Luna, ZE Maite), one (1) disposal area for ballast and clays
washed (ZD Neptuno), one (1) vegetal soil collection area (ZAV), a processing plant
clays (the Plant) and a stockpile; Likewise, it contemplates a 15 kV electrical line and its connection to a
existing line and the construction and/or improvement of internal roads to access the different areas.
In this context, the present study is framed in the characterization of the human environment component, the
which considered the human groups close to the Penco commune Project, as this is where
The closest population or one that is related to the Project area is located.
In this way, the life systems and customs of the human groups (SVCGH) present were analyzed.
in the Area of Influence for the Human Environment (AIMH). The characterization was carried out through the description
of the geographical, demographic, anthropological, socioeconomic, and social well-being dimensions
basic; analyzing aspects related to culture
culture, traditions, organization and social reality of its
inhabitants. Aspects such as economic activities, connectivity, access to goods were also considered.
and information technologies, adjusting each of the themes to the demands of the Information System.
Environmental Impact Assessment (SEIA).
In this last aspect, the study sought to provide technical and conceptual arguments that allow,
subsequently, in accordance with the provisions of articles 7 and 8 of the D.S. No. 40/2012, of the MMA, and its
modifications1, Regulation of the Environmental Impact Assessment System (RSEIA), estimate the impact
that could generate the execution of the Project in the life systems and customs of human groups

Prepare a characterization of the Life Systems and Customs of Human Groups that are part of the
Area of Influence for the Human Environment, based on the five constituent dimensions of the environment

Article 11 of Law 19,300 and that could be associated with significant adverse effects or alterations on
the quality of the life systems and customs of human groups.

3.1.3 Methodology
Regarding the methodology used to prepare this LBMH, a study was chosen
descriptive, which sought to provide a holistic vision of the reality experienced by the inhabitants of the area of
influence; Due to the above, it was decided to use a mixed methodology, which combines the collection
and analysis of quantitative and qualitative information.
3.1.3.1 Methodological Approach
As mentioned, the research design is descriptive in nature, as the study refers or narrates
characteristics and properties of an object, subject or specific situation. In this case, it is
identify and detail the characteristics and lifestyles of the population present in the area of influence
of the Project, without using value judgments and seeking high levels of objectivity, observing and
describing the social phenomenon as it presents itself in its context and time space.
Likewise, the methodological approach used to prepare the LBMH corresponds to a
mixed type research, in which the integration of qualitative and quantitative approaches is combined
throughout the research and analysis process.

The mixed approach can be understood as “(…) a process that collects, analyzes and provides data
quantitative and qualitative, in the same study” (Tashakkori and Teddlie, 2003, cited in Barrantes 2014,
p.100). In this way, it seeks to provide both an explanation of the facts (quantitative approach) and a
understanding of these (qualitative approach), helping to eliminate possible research biases
and strengthen the investigative process (Barrantes, 2014, p. 98).
The qualitative approach pays attention to the meanings, interpretations and subjectivity of the
people in social life, connecting through qualitative research techniques on perception
of the self in the social context (Canales, 2006). This methodology not only investigates the conditions
of life and perceptions about the human environment and the territory, but rather resorts to secondary information
in terms of statistics and administrative data (quantitative approach) to put into context
different sources of information.
It should be noted that, in a research with a mixed approach, both the quantitative approach and the
qualitative are important and valuable, none prevails over the other; On the contrary, they work
jointly, which allows us to understand the reality being studied in a more comprehensive way. By
the above, the mixed approach should not be understood as a mixture in which the characteristics
particulars of each approach are erased or become relative. On the contrary, from a perspective
eclectic, the richness of mixed research consists of taking advantage of the benefits and strengths of each
focus.
Consequently, the collection of information
rmation has been carried out through the use of both
qualitative and quantitative methodologies, seeking to ensure that the background information presented achieves
approach local reality through: (i) primary sources based on field work; and (ii)
secondary sources, through the analysis of available information. All of the above to account for and
characterize the AI in order to subsequently evaluate the environmental aspects to which they refer
Articles 7 and 8 of the RSEIA.
The methodological stages of the study, consistent with the specific objectives set, were: (a)
Preliminary identification of the area of influence of the Project to guide the collection of information and
subsequently advance in an iterative process of confirming the area of influence of the study, (b)
development of field campaigns to collect information from primary sources, (c) description of
the life systems and customs of human groups through secondary and primary sources.
Thus, the methodological procedure to carry out the characterization of the Environment component
Human considered four types of activities; namely: (i) previous activities, (ii) collection of
secondary information on the study area and background information related to the Project, (iii) field work,
and (iv) office work.

In accordance with the preceding description, the area of influence has been defined and justified based on the
definition referred to in article 2 letter a) of the RSEIA, which describes it as “The geographical area or space,
whose attributes, natural or sociocultural elements must be considered in order to define
if the project or activity generates or presents any of the effects, characteristics or circumstances of the
article 11 of the law, or to justify the existence of said effects, characteristics or circumstances.”
Likewise, the provisions of Article 18 of the RSEIA on minimum contents of the
Environmental Impact Studies, referred to Article 18 letter d) and that defines or justifies the area of influence
“The determination and justification of the area of influence of the project or activity, including a
general description of it. The area of influence will be defined and justified for each affected element.
of the environment, taking into consideration potentially significant environmental impacts
about them, as well as the geographical space in which the parts, works and/or actions of the
project or activity.
Consequently, the Area of Influence of the Human Environment (AIMH) was defined considering all possible
alteration to the environment, and specifically to the Human Environment component, product of the execution
of the Project. For this, the impact of the parts, works and actions of the Project and its
potential alterations on the life systems and customs of the human groups that inhabit
the territory where it is developed, in order to
terminate the presence or absence of impacts
significant in the given area.

In the first aspect, in 1880 the Villa de Penco had one of the most exclusive hotels in the south
of Chile, providing services to presidents Montt, Balmaceda and Alessandri. Furthermore, the proximity to

In terms of trade, in 1840, President José Joaquín Prieto declared the port enabled, generating
an important flow of cargo with international ports such as California. Subsequently, the need
transportation of coal produced in Cosmito, Cerro Verde and Lirquén, led to the construction of the branch
railway from Concepción to Penco, which was inaugurated by President Manuel Balmaceda in the year
1889.
Port and railway connections led to the establishment of industries that were transformed
in a pole of population attraction due to the offer of jobs. In 1886 the Refinery was founded
Sudamericana de Azúcar, which would later merge with the Sugar Refinery Company of
Viña del Mar (CRAV); while in 1898 the Fanaloza National Earthenware Factory was created.
