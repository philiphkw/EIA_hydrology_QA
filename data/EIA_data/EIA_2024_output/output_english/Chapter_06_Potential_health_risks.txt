6 POTENTIAL RISKS THAT THE PROJECT OR ACTIVITY COULD GENERATE IN
PEOPLE'S HEALTH
Supreme Decree No. 40/2012 of the Ministry of the Environment, Regulation of the Evaluation System
of Environmental Impact, states in its article 18, letter h) that “When the project or activity must
present an Environmental Impact Study for generating risk to the health of the population to which it refers
letter a) of article 11 of the Law, and there was no primary Quality or Emission Standard in Chile or in the
reference states indicated in article 11 of this Regulation, the proponent must consider
a specific chapter related to the potential risks that the project or activity could generate in the
health of people.”
In view of the above, and in accordance with the analysis presented in Chapter 5 on effects,
characteristics or circumstances of article 11 of Law 19,300, the project will generate significant impact
associated with art. 5 of the RSEIA associated with a risk to the health of the population due to the quantity and quality
of effluents, emissions or waste, specifically with regard to literal a) in emissions of
respirable particulate matter MP 2.5, during the operation phase in receptors located within the
area of influence of the project.
Notwithstanding the above, this contaminant is regulated in Chile, an analysis was made based on
the significance criteria established in the Evaluation Criterion in the SEIA: impact of emissions on
zones saturated by respirable particulate matter MP10 and fine respirable particulate matter MP2.5
(First edition. Environmental Evaluation Service, 2023), so the presentation of
a specific chapter related to the potential risks that the project or activity could generate in the
health of people within the framework established in article 18, literal h).
