7.1 Introduction
In accordance with the provisions of Law No. 19,300 on General Bases of the Environment (modified by
Law No. 20,417/10) and article 18 letter i) and article
or 97 of the D.S. N°40/2012 of the Ministry of the Environment
Environment, and its modifications, Regulation of the Environmental Impact Assessment System (in
hereinafter RSEIA), which establish that an Environmental Impact Study must consider a Plan
of Mitigation, Repair and Compensation Measures that will describe and justify the measures that will be
will be adopted to eliminate, minimize, repair, restore or compensate, as appropriate and when
appropriate, the adverse environmental effects of the Project described in Chapter 5 of this EIA.
Without prejudice to these measures, it is necessary to point out that the Project has considered, as part of the
design criteria and operating procedures, environmental management measures and standards
aimed at avoiding and/or controlling non-significant adverse environmental impacts in their different phases
of execution; The above is reflected in Chapter 1, Project Description. Likewise, in the
Chapter 13 Voluntary Environmental Commitments of this EIA, a set of actions is presented
voluntary environmental management, aimed at controlling effects or annoyances towards the community,
control non-significant impacts and promote or enhance certain benefits expected from the Project.
In this regard, the aforementioned literal i) of Article 18 of the RSEIA, states, in relation to the contents to be presented
in preparing an EIA:
A Mitigation, Repair and Compensation Measures Plan that will describe and justify the
measures to be taken to eliminate, minimize, repair, restore or compensate for the effects
adverse environmental consequences of the project or activity described in letter g) of this article. The Plan
must comply with the provisions of Paragraph 1 of Title VI of this Regulation.
For its part, paragraph 1 of Title VI of the RSEIA, referring to the Plan of Mitigation, Repair and Measures
Environmental Compensation, notes:
Article 97.- Environmental Measures Plan.
The Plan of Environmental Mitigation, Repair and Compensation Measures of a project or
activity must contain, where appropriate, one or more of the following plans:
a) Plan of Environmental Mitigation Measures.
b) Plan of Environmental Repair Measures.
c) Environmental Compensation Measures Plan.

The Plan must contain for each phase of the project or activity the indication of the component
environmental; the associated environmental impact; the type of measurement; name, objective, description and
justification of the corresponding measure; place, form and opportunity of implementation; and the
compliance indicator.
The sole compliance with the applicable environmental regulations will not necessarily constitute a
mitigation, reparation or compensation measure.
For their part, Articles 98, 99 and 100 respectively define the mitigation, repair and
compensation.
Article 98.- Environmental mitigation measures.
The purpose of mitigation measures is to avoid or reduce the adverse effects of the project.
or activity, whatever its execution phase. They will be expressed in a Plan of Measures
Environmental Mitigation that must consider at least one of the following measures:
a) Those that prevent or completely avoid the significant adverse effect, by not
execution of a work or action, or any of its parts.
b) Those that minimize or reduce the significant adverse effect, through adequate
limitation or reduction of the extension, magnitude or duration of the work or action, or of any
of its parts.
c) Those that minimize or reduce the significant adverse effect through measures
technological and/or management considered in the design.
Article 99.- Environmental repair measures.
Repair measures are intended to replace one or more of the components or elements
of the environment to a quality similar to that which it had prior to the impact on said
component or element or, if this is not possible, restore its basic properties.
Article 100.- Environmental compensation measures.
Compensation measures are intended to produce or generate an alternative positive effect
and equivalent to an effect
identified adverse event that cannot be mitigated or repaired.
These measures will include, among others, the replacement of natural resources or elements of the
environment affected by others of similar characteristics, class, nature, quality and
function.
Regarding the places where mitigation and repair measures will be adopted, these will occur in
areas or places in which significant impacts occur or generate on the elements of the environment
environment. In the case of compensation measures, they will take place in areas or places where the

7.3.2 Measurement Plan for the Flora and Vegetation Component
i. Significant Impacts
Considering the impact evaluation analysis carried out in Chapter 4 of this EIA, the activity
Vegetation clearance is the main action that generates impacts on the flora and vegetation component.
As a result of this activity, the following significant impacts were established:
a) IFV-02 Loss of flora specimens
This impact is manifested by the activities of clearing vegetation in natural environments and
intervened, resulting in the loss of flora specimens due to the materialization of the Project.
ii. Definition of measurements
