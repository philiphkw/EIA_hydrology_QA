8 CONTINGENCIES AND EMERGENCIES PREVENTION PLAN
8.1 Introduction
The Mining Development Project of Clay Extraction for the Production of Earth Concentrate
Raras, hereinafter, without distinction, the “Project” corresponds to the set of parts, works and actions
that will allow the construction, operation and closure of a mining site for extraction and processing
of clays, a material present in the area, which, due to natural effects, is highly
weathered, with the presence of rare earths in clay minerals. The Project will be developed
mainly in the commune of Penco, Province of Concepción, Biobío Region, without prejudice to part
of the works are located in the commune of Concepción, on the border with the Commune of Penco.
The Project considers the following facilities: one (1) Processing Plant, three (3) storage areas
clay extraction (ZE V
ictoria Norte, ZE Luna, ZE Maite), one (1) disposal area for ballast and clays
processed (ZD Neptuno), one (1) vegetal soil collection area (ZAV), a processing plant
clays (the “Plant”), a stockpile for the temporary collection of material prior to processing, a line
electricity and its connection to the existing line and the construction of internal roads to access the different
areas. The Processing Plant will have a capacity to process up to 320 tons/hour of
material to produce Rare Earth concentrate. In addition, the modification of a section is considered
existing approximately 1.22 kilometers of the Hualpén – San Fernando pipeline, owned by
ENAP, reason for the exploitation of the Maite ZE. The Project in turn includes the implementation of works
temporary corresponding to work facilities for the construction and authorization of the works of the
Project.
8.2 Objectives
In accordance with the provisions of Title III, letter j) of Article 18 of the D.S. 40/2012, modified by
D.S. 30/2023, both from the Ministry of the Environment, Regulation of the Evaluation System of

Contingencies and Emergencies, in accordance with the provisions of Paragraph 2 of Title VI of the aforementioned
RSEIA.

8.6.1 Description of Identified Risks
Natural Hazards
They are all those caused by natural phenomena in which the
integrity of the worker, the infrastructure or the continuity of the processes.
These risks are made up of three essential factors; The first is the danger that refers to
the probability of a certain natural phenomenon, of a certain extent, intensity and duration. The
Second is vulnerability, which refers to the impact of the phenomenon on the organization, and the
Third is responsiveness.
Regarding the consideration of climate risks related to rising temperatures, the
Climate risks are evaluated at low levels or with slight increases in the commune of Penco, so they are not
Its consideration in this Contingency and Emergency Prevention Plan is considered applicable.
• Earthquakes and tsunamis
The Project is located in an area with seismic zoning “Zone 2” according to the NCh. 433 “Design
“building seismic”, which implies a high probability of occurrence for this event compared to
with other zones defined for the rest of Chile. Therefore, “earthquake risk” is identified in all
the
stages of the project.
According to the information presented in the “Tsunami Flood Letter of Bahías Concepción and San
Vicente, referring to the event of the year 1835; Prepared by SHOA”, the simulation result shows the
Project area outside the flood zone; Therefore, it is not considered a tsunami risk.

8.7.3 Organization of the emergency
8.7.3.1 Emergency Levels
Depending on the severity of the emergency, the means of protection, human and material, are determined.
that must be mobilized to face emergency situations that may occur in the
facilities; There are three Emergency Levels.
Level 1 or Emergency Attempt: Situation in which the risk or accident that causes it can be
neutralized simply and quickly, with the available means and resources present at the time and
accident site.
Level 2 or Partial Emergency: Situation in which the risk or accident requires the
intervention of teams specifically designated and instructed for this purpose; affects an area of the
facilities and “partial evacuation” or evacuation of the affected area may be necessary.
Level 3 or General Emergency: Situation in which the risk or accident endangers safety and
physical integrity of the people and it is necessary to proceed with the eviction or evacuation, abandoning the
facilities. Requires the intervention of External Help.
It must be highlighted that the same accident can, due to its evolution, lead to different Levels of
Emergency.

8.11 First aid
It is the first response to unwanted events that endanger a person's life. everything
Effort made must be executed causing the least possible impact.
Only properly trained personnel will be able to provide first aid to an injured person. The
General procedure of action in case of giving first aid will be as follows:
- Stay calm and act quickly and safely.
- Evaluate the scene of the accident and verify the condition of the affected person.
- Handle the affected person gently and cautiously.
- Reassure the injured person.
- Give notice as quickly as possible asking for help, indicating the greatest number of
information.
- Do not remove the injured person unless his life is in danger (fire, electrocution,
collapses, pollution, asphyxiation, drowning, etc.).
- Consider the control of bleeding and breathing as a priority, provided that the elements
that make up the first aid kit allow it and do not put yourself at greater risk by
crashed
- If there is loss of consciousness, do not give drink.
- Cover the injured person so that they do not get cold, preventing thermal shock.
- For transfer, do it carefully (immobilization, stretcher, etc.)
