1 PLAN FOR COMPLIANCE WITH APPLICABLE ENVIRONMENTAL LEGISLATION (ART.
18º L)
1.1 Introduction
In accordance with the provisions of letter g) of Article 12 of Law No. 19,300 and its modifications and in
letter l) of Article 18 of the D.S. N°40/2012, MMA, and its modifications, SEIA Regulations, this
Chapter indicates the environmental legislation applicable to the Environmental Impact Study of the “Project of
Mining Development of Clay Extraction for the Production of Rare Earth Concentrate” and the form
in which this will be fulfilled.
These articles establish that the Environmental Impact Studies must contemplate a “plan of
compliance with applicable environmental legislation” where environmental regulations are identified and analyzed
applicable to the Project and the way in which the obligations contained in said
identified standards.
Within the framework of the Environmental Impact Assessment System (SEIA), the concept of legislation
applicable environmental protection considers those standards whose objective is to ensure the protection of the environment
environment, the preservation of nature and the conservation of environmental heritage, and impose a
obligation or requirement whose
Compliance must be accredited by the Owner of the Project or activity,
during the evaluation process.
In the SEIA, the environmental regulations applicable to the Project are composed of the standards
current that each environmental component has. In this set of standards, there are quality standards
primary and secondary environmental and emission standards to air, water and soil; Furthermore, they all integrate
standards that have an environmental component, whether expressed in compliance with standards,
in the requirements to comply with the environmental permits established in Title VII of the Regulations of the
SEIA, or in the regulation on the management, prohibition or use of natural resources, and in general, of the
elements of the environment. The set of environmental regulations applicable to the Project is
determined case by case, since it depends on the type of Project, the effects it generates or presents and the
location of this one.
The identification of the environmental standards applicable to the Project has been determined according to the
impacts that their activities produce on a certain environmental component. In this way,
For each of the standards identified as applicable to the Project, the regulated matter is indicated.
Likewise, the name, associated environmental aspect and organism from which it emanated, a brief
description of the content of the standard and compliance with the provisions contained in
each one of them. All this, with the aim of presenting the information in a systematic and orderly manner.
