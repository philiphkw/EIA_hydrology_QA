11 RELATIONSHIP WITH REGIONAL AND COMMUNAL DEVELOPMENT PLANS
Law No. 19,300 on General Environmental Bases, modified by Law No. 20,417, establishes
in its Article 9 ter that the proponents must describe the way in which their projects or activities
“are related to regional development policies, plans and programs, as well as to
community development.”
Additionally, the Regulation of the Environmental Impact Assessment System, Supreme Decree No. 40
of the year 2012, modified by D.S. No. 30/2023, both of the MMA, establishes, in its Title III, Article 13,
the following:
“The proponents of the projects or activities, in their Studies or Impact Statements
Environmental, they must describe the way in which their projects or activities relate to the
regional development policies, plans and programs, as well as development plans
community, the long-term climate strategy, the sectoral mitigation and adaptation plans,
regional and communal climate change action plans, strategic plans for
water resources in the basin, and sectoral plans for disaster risk management, in the area
influence of the project, which have been previously approved and are in force,
as appropriate
To evaluate the way in which the project or activity relates to the indicated instruments
In the previous section, the owner must indicate whether the typology of the project or activity is
recognized in any of the strategic definitions, general objectives or specific objectives
of said instruments. Likewise, you must indicate which of these definitions and objectives
are benefited or harmed by the project.
The owner must indicate whether the design of the project or activity, its measures and commitments
voluntary environmental measures are compatible with the measures established in said instruments.”

community development plans, the following steps have been deployed:

communal relevant to the analysis;
• Identification of the strategic guidelines or general objectives of each instrument, in order to
to determine if the typology of the Project is recognized in any of the definitions
strategic, general objectives or specific objectives; and,

11.1 Relationship with regional development policies, plans and programs
11.1.1 Regional Development Strategy Biobío Region (2015-2030)
The Regional Development Strategy (ERD) is a planning instrument in which the government
regional - based on a diagnosis that considers the central trends, characteristics and/or dynamics
of the region - builds a shared strategic discourse regarding an objective image that is
aims to achieve within a certain time horizon, defining the strategic orientations, the
central axes and objectives for development. From this point of view, it constitutes the common thread
of public actions and coordination with the private sector to materialize the objectives that
allow us to achieve said objective image.
The ERD of the Biobío Region aims to strengthen sustainable development, ensuring that the benefits
of the economic growth that the Region experiences reach all its inhabitants. Defines how
objective-projected image for the year 2030 the following:
a) Be a leading region, recognized nationally and internationally for its sustainability, collaboration,
competitiveness, inclusion and social equity.
b) It bases its development, dynamism and opportunities on its inhabitants, wealth of its resources
natural resources, identities, historical prominence, recognizing and valuing their heritage, diversity
cultural and creativity, promoting the generation of social capital.
c) It is inserted in the world, privileging the generation of knowledge, research, technology,
talent attraction, innovation and entrepreneurship; based on its generating condition
clean energy, logistics, port and maritime, promoting the productive, agri-food and
sustainable forestry.
d) Cultivate democratic, participatory and decentralized governance, favoring territories
creative and integrated, with efficient infrastructure and connectivity.
e) Creates value, promoting high levels of quality of life and opportunities, so that people
develop their aspirations, dreams and capabilities.
To respond to the basic challenges of the region and achieve the target image projected for the year
2030, various guidelines are proposed with their respective objectives. For each of these objectives,

11.1.2 Strategy and Regional Political Action Plan for Conservation and Us

Sustainable Biodiversity of the Biobío Region 2017-2030.
The Biobío Region concentrates a great productive activity based on the historical use of its resources
natural, these being the basis of the regional economy. This anthropization has caused various
impacts on our environment, bringing with it the degradation of regional ecosystems,
the ecosystem services that they provide us and, therefore, the quality of life of the inhabitants of the
Region.
The Strategy and Regional Political Action Plan for the Conservation and Sustainable Use of Biodiversity
of the Biobío Region 2017-2030, is a public instrument that aims to direct the actions of
conservation of the biodiversity of the region during the period 2017 and 2030. This has been prepared by
the Natural Resources and Biodiversity Area of the Seremi del Medio Ambiente of the Biobío Region in
jointly with the Planning and Regional Development Division of the Regional Government of Biobío.

11.1.3 Environmental Policy of the Biobío Region
The Environmental Policy of the Biobío Region is nourished by two main guidelines. The first, those
challenges that from the environmental point of view derive from the Regional Development Strategy and, the
second, the local expression of the National Environmental Policy.
Environmental Policy must materialize in a heterogeneous regional context marked by a great
diversity of ecological areas, productive vocations, social structures and levels of development. By
For this reason, the authorities and communities of each of the regions of the country have assumed the decision to
develop the respective environmental policy that specifies the diagnoses, challenges and tasks that must be
be addressed in each of the places where it will rule.
The Policy seeks to make the development process environmentally sustainable, ensuring the right to
living in a pollution-free environment, protecting the environment, preserving the
nature and the conservation of environmental heritage. Its ultimate goal is the quality of life for everyone.
Chileans and future generations.
The National Environmental Policy and its regional expression is based on three major foundations that
inspire:
First of all, there is the quality of life of people. Sustainable development translates into a process
of sustained and equitable improvement in people's quality of life, based on measures
appropriate conservation and protection of the environment, so as not to compromise the
expectations of future generations.
Second, the complementarity between socioeconomic development and environmental sustainability. The
Economic growth must be compatible with the preservation of the environment, since only then can
guarantees that resources for development will be available in quantity and quality. That is, the
satisfaction of the needs of the current population, however pressing they may be, should not
compromising the environmental resources available to future generations.
Finally, the Regional Environmental Policy seeks social equity and overcoming poverty. The
Sustainable development seeks to integrate social goals with economic and environmental goals in the
