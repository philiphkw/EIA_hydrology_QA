12.1 Introduction
Article 15 of D.S N°40/2012, of the Ministry of the Environment, Regulation of the System of
Environmental Impact Assessment, and its modifications (hereinafter RSEIA), establishes that “projects
or activities subject to the Environmental Impact Assessment System must always consider the
“strategically evaluated policies and plans, in accordance with the law.” To do this, the owner “must
identify strategically evaluated policies and plans that are relevant, as well as the
compatibility of the project or activity with the use of the territory and the environmental objectives of such
policies and plans.”
For its part, transitional article 2 of the RSEIA indicates that “for the purposes of what is established in letter g) of the
article 3 and in paragraph 2 of article 15 of this Regulation, it is
will consider evaluated
strategically, in accordance with the provisions of paragraph 1 bis of Title II of the Law, the plans
qualified through the Environmental Impact Assessment System prior to entry into
validity of Law No. 20,417, as well as the plans that have been in force since before the issuance
of Law No. 19,300”.
Thus, below, the relationship with the Project is indicated and those Policies and plans
strategically evaluated, as well as the territorial compatibility of the Project with the instruments that
regulate the use of the territory where it will be located.
12.2 Objectives
The objective of this chapter corresponds to describing and analyzing the existing relationship of the Project with
the plans and policies strategically evaluated in the Biobío Region and, in particular, the communes of
Penco and Concepción.

12.3 Methodology
This chapter develops the territorial compatibility of the Project with the use of the territory and the
relationship with the objectives and/or guidelines of the strategically evaluated policies and plans, of
in accordance with what is indicated in paragraph 1 bis of Law No. 19,300 and articles No. 15 and transitional article 2

• Strategically Evaluated Policies and Plans: for this analysis, the public registry of
EAE, available on the virtual portal of the Ministry of the Environment1, which makes known the
documentation associated with the application of the SEA in Policies, Plans, Instruments of
Territorial Planning (IPT), Coastal Edge Zoning (ZBC), among others. For each record
The administrative acts that are part of the evaluation process are exposed.
• Current plans: This analysis verifies the relationship of the project with the Instruments of
Territorial Planning (IPT) in force in the location area, and - as the article points out
2nd transition of the aforementioned RSEIA -, the plans will be considered strategically evaluated
qualified through the Environmental Impact Assessment System prior to entry
in force of Law No. 20,417, as well as the plans that have been in force since before the
issuance of Law No. 19,300. The identification and review of these plans was carried out through the
existing platforms such as the MINVU Urban Observatory, and Evaluation Service
Environmental, respectively.

12.4.1.1 Chilean Energy Policy 2050 and its update
The current Energy Policy of Chile is the result of a participatory process promoted by the
Ministry of Energy, which had as its objective the definition and formulation of a long-term Policy
that had social, political and technical validation. This process, called “Energy 2050”, is
structured into four stages of development and three segments of participation (participation at levels
political-strategic with the Advisory Committee; at the technical, expert and sector level involved in
energy with the themed tables; and at a level that includes the entire population, with the platform
citizen). This led to the subsequent approval by the Ministry of Energy through the Decree
N°148/2015 and published in the Official Gazette on February 29, 2016.
The National Energy Policy was updated in 2020 and has a termination resolution dated
02/22/2022 of the Ministry of Energy, which is why the relationship analysis is carried out based on said
update.
Within the Energy Policy, it stands out that:
The first purpose is the desire to be protagonists of climate ambition, not only
to fulfill the country's commitments, but to contribute to confronting the global climate crisis. The
historic commitment that we set to achieve carbon neutrality before 2050 will bring
benefits for society and essentially, for people.
And they are the ones who motivate our second purpose: we are convinced that the transition
energy must be explored to improve people's quality of life. to have cities
clean, to reach all homes with energy, and to move in a sustainable way. The politics
energy puts the person at the center.
The third purpose seeks to change the productive identity of the country, integrating clean energies into
every process and activity of our economy. When we clean up energy, we clean up mining,
agriculture, transportation, industry... and we make our economy, exporting and open to the
world, be more competitive in a planet more aware of the environmental footprint of the products we

ation of Decree No. 148 of the PRCC Ordinance, which
