13 VOLUNTARY ENVIRONMENTAL COMMITMENTS
13.1 Introduction
In accordance with the provisions of Letter m) of Article 18 of Supreme Decree No. 40/12 of the
Ministry of the Environment, Regulation of the Environmental Impact Assessment System (in
hereinafter the RSEIA), and its modifications, the Environmental Impact Studies must consider:
“the description of the content of those voluntary environmental commitments, not
required by current legislation, that the owner of the project or activity contemplates
carried out, with the precise indication of the place and time in which they will be verified, as well as
compliance indicators, if applicable. Among these commitments, it will be possible
consider those that take charge of non-significant impacts and those associated with
verify that no significant impacts are generated.”
In this way, this Chapter presents the Voluntary Environmental Commitments of the
Project contemplated by the Project Owner, which are oriented towards two lines of action:
agreements that the Owner will implement that are not necessarily associated with impacts (such as
for example those related to early citizen participation or others of a general nature), and
commitments arising from environmental impacts of a “Non-Significant” nature (Chapter 4
Prediction and Environmental Impact Assessment).
13.1.1 Objective
The purpose of this document is to present the characteristics and scope that the
Voluntary Environmental Commitments (CAV) considered by the Project.

CAV-05 Germplasm Conservation of Gomortega keule, Citronella mucronata and Pitavia punctata
Description:
The commitment considers the rescue of genetic material to carry out a recovery plan
ex situ conservation, maintaining part of the local populations of Gomortega
keule, Citronella mucronata and Pitavia punctata outside the area of origin. As a criterion
of additionality and faced with a climate change scenario, it is proposed
build a variety garden in a town located further south, outside the area of
current occupation of the three species, with fewer water restrictions, but with
thermal stability due to proximity to bodies of water. The variety garden corresponds
to an ex situ conservation practice, where the adaptation of populations will be evaluated
of Gomortega keule, Citronella mucronata and Pitavia punctata and the
cultural care necessary to have a representative replica of the
genetic variability of the local population where the Project will be located.
Justification:
The proposed measure aims to contribute to the increase in the number of CCP individuals in the
sector committed to maintaining the genetic base of its populations, for this
contemplates the collection of germplasm of origin for propagation from
individuals present in the area of influence of the Project.
Sexual and asexual propagation will be implemented in order to obtain a number
suitable plants for in situ research and enrichment programs.
Additionally, the owner proposes an ex situ conservation bank, in order to expand the
presence of these species to other localities.
Location:
The rescue of germplasm (fruits/seeds and cuttings), of the species in category
queule, naranjillo and pitao, will be made from selected individuals in areas of
native preservation forest, or in surrounding areas in the area of influence of the
Project, without affecting the survival or reproductive capacity of the individuals in the
Obtaining propagation material.
