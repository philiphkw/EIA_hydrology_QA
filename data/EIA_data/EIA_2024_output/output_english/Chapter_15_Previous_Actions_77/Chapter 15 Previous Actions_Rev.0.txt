15.1 Summary
The purpose of this chapter is to describe the activities that Aclara (REE UNO SPA), hereinafter “The
Owner", developed in the Early Citizen Participation (PCT) process, according to the "guide for
early citizen participation in projects that are presented to the SEIA” prepared by
the Environmental Evaluation Service in November 2023, for the “Mining Development Project of
Extraction of Clays for the Production of Rare Earth Concentrates”, hereinafter “the Project”. The
PCT carried out by the company voluntarily was aimed at citizens and the local community,
also motivating the participation of civil society organizations, authorities and local governments
(municipalities, public services, regional governments or others).
Based on the above, the Owner implemented a dialogue plan with the citizens of Penco and
Conception, areas of influence of the project, considering the principles of transparency and good faith with
the purpose of facilitating the delivery of general and specific project information, and at the same time
raise comments, observations and suggestions for a participatory design of the project in a way
early upon entry to SEIA.
During planning, a scenario diagnosis was carried out that considered analyzing characteristics
social, historical and cultural of the inhabitants of Penco through the collection of information
through (1) bibliographic review, (2) systematic monitoring of citizen opinion through
social networks, focused mainly on Penco, and (3) a perception study prepared by the
Ekhos consulting firm. The above made it possible to identify that a large part of the citizens wanted and needed
learn more about the Project, and that he valued the spaces for dialogue about the project.
The development of Early Citizen Participation was carried out by inviting citizens to inform themselves
through a campaign to call and disseminate information on radio, written press, television and networks
social.
More than 1,000 people participated in PCT activities, including citizens, dir
neighborhood people,
opinion leaders and local, regional and national authorities. PCT activities: (1) Meetings
with Citizenship in which 382 people participated in various meetings and 3 citizen assemblies
in which 113 people signed assistance and requests by lobbying law to 68 communal authorities,
regional and national, (2) a Demonstration Center was also available to inform
experiential about the process to which 434 visits were observed Demonstrative Center; (3) Enabling a
Permanent Open House, on Las Heras Street #565, commune of Penco, meters from the main square,
registering an average weekly traffic of 100 visitors and where 37 meetings were held with more
of 304 participants of which 267 signed attendance.

15.4 Objectives of Early Citizen Participation
15.4.1 General Objective
Provide citizens with access to technical and environmental information about the project, considering its
contributions and opinions made within the framework of the Early Citizen Participation (PCT) process,
developed by the company between July 2023 and February 2024 prior to its entry into the
Environmental Impact Assessment (SEIA).
The above with the purpose of making an early approach that allows the Owner to lift
observations, resolve doubts or comments, in order to develop a project aligned with the
expectations of citizens, incorporating an opportunity to promote relations of
cooperation, understanding and trust between Aclara and the communities of Penco and the Biobío Region.
15.4.2 Specific Objectives
• Collect pertinent information from the territory that allows designing and implementing a process of
Early Citizen Participation according to the socio-cultural characteristics of your area
of influence.
• Create relationships of trust that allow the Project to increase dialogue with the
local communities and that they have an impact on the project.
• Inform communities about the Project in a transparent, relevant and timely manner.
• Identify concerns and doubts of the communities regarding the project and clear them before
become complex or conflictive situations.
• Incorporate local knowledge into the Environmental Impact Study, as well as suggestions or
corresponding observations that are founded and feasible to be accepted.
15.5 Methodology
The Early Citizen Participation (PCT) process, as already noted, was organized with the objective
to present the background and scope of the EIA to the local community (authorities, organizations and
natural persons), to then receive observations and suggestions to incorporate them into the Project, and
clear up doubts and comments.

15.6.1.2 Scenario Diagnosis and Identification of Social Actors
i. Geographic and demographic characteristics
The commune of Penco has a total area of 107.6 km2. Bordering to the north with the communes of
Tomé, to the south with Concepción, to the east with Florida, to the west with Talcahuano and Concepción Bay.
According to the Communal Development Plan (Pladeco) 2020-2027, it has a 99% urban population,
concentrated in two coastal sectors, Penco and Lirquén.
According to the 2017 Census, it has a population of 47,367 inhabitants, which are distributed 52.25%
women, being 24,750 people, and 47.75% men with a total of 22,617 people (INE, 2018). The
average age of the inhabitants of Penco is 36 years, with a greater proportion of people
between 45 to 64 years old (INE, 2021).
ii. Economic Activity
In relation to economic activity, it is characterized by its seal as a port city, being not only a point
northern access to the region, but also as a logistical connection point to port activity
regional through route 152. At the same time, it is characterized by the strong presence of the forestry sector to the point
that, more than half of the communal surface is made up of forest plantations (Agricultural Census
and Forestal, 2021).
In business terms and according to information collected from the Internal Revenue Service (SII, 2021),
Penco is home to 2,060 companies, of which 1,317 correspond to microenterprises and 291 to small ones.
companies, according to statistics from the Internal Revenue Service (SII) from 2021. Although it only has 22
large companies, these employ a considerable 32% of dependent workers. The economy
The local area focuses mainly on wholesale and retail trade, representing 37% of the
companies. This is followed by the transportation and storage sectors (15%) and the manufacturing industry.
(10%) (SII, 2021).
