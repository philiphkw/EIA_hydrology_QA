ENVIRONMENTAL IMPACT STUDY OF THE MINING DEVELOPMENT PROJECT
EXTRACTION OF CLAYS FOR PRODUCTION OF RARE EARTH CONCENTRATE
Owner: REE Uno SpA; Legal Representative: Nelson Horacio Donoso Navarrete
The Mining Development Project for Clay Extraction for the Production of Rare Earth Concentrate,
hereinafter, interchangeably, the “Project”, corresponds to the set of parts, works and actions that
will allow the construction, operation and closure of a mining site for the extraction and processing of
clays, a material present in the area, which, due to natural effects, is highly weathered, with
presence of rare earths in clay minerals. The Project will be developed mainly in the commune
of Penco, Province of Concepción, Biobío Region, without prejudice to the fact that part of the works are located in the
commune of Concepción, on the border with the Commune of Penco.
The Project considers the following facilities: three clay extraction areas (called Victoria
Norte, Luna and Maite), a disposal area for ballast and washed clays (called Neptuno), an area
of vegetal soil collection, a clay processing plant, a stockpile for the temporary collection of
material prior to processing, an electrical line and its connection to an existing line and the construction of
Internal roads to access the different areas. The Processing Plant will have a capacity to
process up to 320 tons/hour of material to produce Rare Earth concentrate. Furthermore, it
considers the modification of an existing section of approximately 1.22 kilometers of an oil pipeline
property of ENAP. The Project in turn has the implementation of corresponding temporary works
to work facilities for the construction and authorization of the Project works.
The Project enters the Environmental Impact Assessment System (SEIA) because it corresponds
to a mining development project aimed at the extraction or benefit of a mining deposit, whose
mineral extraction capacity is greater than five thousand tons per month (5,000 t/month); the above, in
in accordance with what is indicated in article 3, letter i), specifically literal i.1., of D.S No. 40/12 of the Ministry
of the Environment, Regulations of the Environmental Impact Assessment System, and its modifications.
The main elements of the environment considered in the baseline correspond to the
components of climate and meteorology, air quality, noise, geology and geomorphology, geological risks,
geomorphological and natural, hydrology, hydrogeology, water quality, limnology, pedology, flora and
terrestrial vegetation, fungi and lichens, terrestrial fauna (vertebrates and invertebrates), archaeology,
paleontology, landscape, protected areas and priority sites for conservation, uses of the territory,
Road infrastructure and human environment, whose characterization is superimposed by the works and activities of the
Project, forming the area of influence, in which other projects or activities are also considered
that have a current Environmental Qualification Resolution.
The estimated investment of the Project amounts to 130 million dollars, estimating a useful life of 9
years. After this period, the appropriateness of the closure and dismantling or the
implementation of adjustments to the Project, which consider the improvement of equipment and processes consistent with the
technological changes available to date, aimed at maintaining its operation.
Considering the type of Project and the characteristics of the area of influence, the environmental impacts
identified significant ones correspond to the following:
● Air Quality Component: Affectation of human receptors due to increased concentration of
particulate matter and receptor gases (ICA-01: operation phase).
● Soil Component: Loss of the natural resource soil (ISU-01: construction and operation phase).
● Flora and Vegetation Component: Loss of flora specimens (IFV-02: construction and
operation).

● Vertebral terrestrial fauna component
radar: Loss of specimens of fauna of interest and/or sensitive
(IFA-01: construction and operation phase), Loss and/or modification of habitat of species of interest
and/or sensitive (IFA-02: construction and operation phase).
● Invertebrate terrestrial fauna component: Loss of specimens of fauna of interest and/or sensitive
(IFI-01: construction and operation phase), Loss and/or modification of habitat of species of interest
and/or sensitive (IFI-02: construction and operation phase).
● Human Environment Component: Affectation to the development of cultural and traditional practices of
indigenous organizations along the forest road adjacent to Estero Penco due to restriction of
access through installation of access control points, informative signage and security patrols.
surveillance (IMH-01: Construction, operation and closure), Restriction of access to natural resources
(medicinal herbs) used by the GHPPI for medicinal and cultural purposes, making it difficult to
manifestation of cultural activities and collective interests, through the installation of control points
access, informative signage and surveillance patrols (IMH-03: Construction, operation and closure)
In order to mitigate, compensate and/or repair the effects of the Project, the Owner proposes a series of
measures during the different phases of the Project, which correspond to the following:
● Air Quality Component: Change of folder in 1.6 km of shared access to project
CEMARC (MC-01), Increase in emissions control by 3.5 km for shared access road to
CEMARC (MC-02), Replacement of heating equipment and stoves that use wood as fuel
(MC-03).
● Flora and vegetation component: Rescue and relocation of 20 specimens of Citronella mucronata
(MM-01), Germplasm collection, nurseryization and replanting of Asplenium trilobum individuals,
Gardoquia multiflora and Vestia foetida (MC-04).
● Terrestrial vertebrate fauna component: Rescue and relocation plan for vertebrate fauna (MM-
02), Controlled disturbance plan (MM-03), Revegetation and conservation of intervened areas
ZD and ZE with native vegetation (MR-01), and Protection of areas surrounding the project (MC-05).
● Terrestrial invertebrate fauna component: Rescue and relocation plan for invertebrate fauna
(MM-04).
● Soil component: Rescue, collection and reuse of soil in intervened areas (MR-02) and
Improvement of soil properties in intervened area (MR-03).
● Human Environment Component: Controlled access protocol for GHPPI (MM-05), Management Plan
information and communication with GHPPI (MM-06) and Support Plan for strengthening practices
cultural activities of the GHPPI (MM-07)
