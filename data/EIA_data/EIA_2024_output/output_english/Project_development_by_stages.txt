As indicated in the introduction to this Chapter, the Project that is presented for evaluation to
through this EIA was adjusted with respect to its initial design due to the presence of
sensitive environmental elements, especially due to the presence of plant species in the category

original. In order to address such a situation and understanding that this will require adjustments in the

To the extent that the first extraction areas are exploited, it is proposed to carry out the project by

Once all applicable permits have been approved and processed, it will be possible to begin its execution

Environmental Impact Assessment. However, the rule adds that what is stated will not apply
previously when the proponent certifies that the project or activity corresponds to one whose
Execution will be carried out in stages.

1.6.3 Estimated duration of each stage
The useful life of the Project that is presented for evaluation through this EIA, corresponding to
stage 1 of the comprehensive project; is given by the time covered by the construction phase, the
operation and the closing phase. The construction phase of the Project considers a duration of
order of 25 months; The operation phase corresponds to 6 years (72 months) and the closing phase
comprises 12 months; That is, it is estimated that, considering all phases, the useful life of the project,
in its stage 1, it is 9 years old.
Regarding the second stage, whose objective is to provide operational continuity to the processing of
rare earths through the exploitation of other areas, will maximize the useful life of the Project,
estimating an approximate duration of 8 additional years (construction, operation and closure). By
Therefore, the second stage of the Project will complement the parts, works and actions of this
Project under evaluation and will be entered into the SEIA in a timely manner for its environmental evaluation.
